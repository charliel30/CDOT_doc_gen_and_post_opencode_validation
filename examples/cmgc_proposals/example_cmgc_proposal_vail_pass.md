## Section 3: Project Understanding

The I-70 West Vail Pass project addresses a critical safety need on one of Colorado's most heavily traveled mountain passes. The eastbound and westbound grades through Vail Pass are steep and winding, with limited sight distance and no auxiliary lanes for slower vehicles. This creates dangerous speed differentials between trucks, recreational vehicles, and passenger cars — particularly during winter when chain-law conditions are in effect.

**Key challenges we have identified:**

1. **High-altitude construction environment** — Work at 9,000–10,600 feet elevation with short construction seasons, extreme weather variability, and reduced equipment productivity
2. **Black Gore Creek and Gore Creek watershed protection** — These are Gold Medal trout fisheries with stringent water quality requirements; any sediment or chemical release would impact aquatic habitat
3. **Avalanche zones** — Multiple avalanche paths cross the I-70 corridor on Vail Pass, constraining winter work and requiring avalanche mitigation during construction
4. **Steep side slopes with limited staging areas** — The narrow canyon limits material storage, equipment staging, and construction access
5. **Peak traffic management** — I-70 through Vail Pass carries 40,000+ vehicles on peak ski weekends; construction cannot impede this critical economic corridor

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach
We will integrate with CDOT's design team to optimize the auxiliary lane alignment for constructability in steep terrain. Our focus areas:
- Minimize cut/fill volumes through alignment refinements
- Identify retaining wall locations where walls are more cost-effective than earthwork
- Evaluate bridge vs. retaining wall solutions at creek crossings
- Develop winter construction plan to extend the productive season

### 4.2 Construction Approach

**Earthwork and Grading**
- GPS-guided machine control for precision grading on steep slopes
- Engineered fill placement in thin lifts with nuclear density verification
- Temporary drainage maintenance throughout grading operations

**Creek Crossings**
- Modular temporary bridge system spanning Black Gore Creek tributaries
- Eliminates temporary stream fills (no Section 404 permit modification needed)
- Allows continuous aquatic passage during construction
- Bridges removed and habitat restored upon permanent structure completion

**Traffic Management**
- No lane closures during peak ski weekends (Friday PM–Sunday PM, November–April)
- Nighttime closures for critical operations (9 PM–5 AM)
- Pilot car operations during single-lane work
- Construction staging coordinated with CDOT's I-70 Mountain Corridor operations center

**High-Altitude/Winter Construction**
- Heated concrete curing enclosures for bridge work
- Ground thaw systems for winter earthwork
- Avalanche monitoring and evacuation protocols
- Equipment winterization program (arctic-grade fluids, heated starts, cold-rated components)

## Section 9: Innovation

### Innovation 1: Modular Temporary Creek Crossing System
**Description:** Pre-fabricated steel bridge panels spanning Black Gore Creek tributaries during construction, avoiding temporary stream fills.
**Benefit:** Eliminates Section 404 permit modifications; protects aquatic habitat; saves estimated 3 months and $2.1M.
**Track record:** Successfully deployed on US 6 Loveland Pass project.

### Innovation 2: Extended-Season Ground Improvement
**Description:** Electric ground-thaw mats installed in advance of spring earthwork, allowing construction to start 3–4 weeks earlier than conventional methods.
**Benefit:** Extends the 6-month construction season to 7+ months; estimated 10% acceleration.
**Track record:** Pilot tested on EJMT approach project; consistent results at 11,000+ feet elevation.
