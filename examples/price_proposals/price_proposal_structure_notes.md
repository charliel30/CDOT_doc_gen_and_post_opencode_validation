# Price Proposal Structure — Key Observations

This document summarizes the pricing patterns observed across CM/GC proposals to help OpenCode understand how to generate the price proposal section.

---

## What a CM/GC Price Proposal Contains

Unlike traditional Design-Bid-Build where the contractor bids the full construction cost, a CM/GC price proposal only covers:

1. **Preconstruction Services Fee** — lump sum for advisory work during design
2. **CM/GC Management Fee Percentage** — the contractor's markup on construction costs
3. **Key Personnel Hourly Rates** — rates for the preconstruction phase

The **full construction price (CAP)** is NOT part of the initial proposal. It is negotiated later after the CM/GC is selected and design reaches 60-90% completion.

---

## Preconstruction Fee Components

| Category | Description | Typical Range (% of total precon fee) |
|----------|-------------|---------------------------------------|
| Constructability Reviews | Reviewing design at 30%, 60%, 90% milestones | 25-30% |
| Cost Estimating / OPCC | Developing cost estimates at each milestone | 20-25% |
| Project Management | Overall coordination and administration | 15-20% |
| Value Engineering | Identifying cost/schedule savings | 8-12% |
| Schedule Development | Building and maintaining CPM schedule | 8-10% |
| Risk Management | Risk identification and tracking | 5-8% |
| Environmental Coordination | Compliance during preconstruction | 3-5% |

---

## Preconstruction Fee Ranges

The preconstruction fee scales with project complexity and value:

| Project Value | Typical Precon Fee Range | % of Construction Value |
|--------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| $50–100M | $500K – $1.5M | 0.5–1.5% |
| $100–500M | $1.5M – $4M | 0.5–1.0% |
| $500M+ | $3M – $6M+ | 0.4–0.7% |

**Floyd Hill example:** $4M precon fee on a $905M project = 0.44%
**Vail Pass example:** $2.8M precon fee (project value not specified in example)

---

## CM/GC Management Fee Percentage

This is the contractor's markup on all direct construction costs and covers:
- Home office overhead
- Profit
- Corporate support (safety, QA, legal, accounting)
- Insurance (excluding project-specific OCIP)

**Typical ranges:**

| Project Size | Fee Range | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| $50–200M | 5.0–7.0% | Higher fee on smaller projects |
| $200–500M | 4.5–6.0% | Mid-range |
| $500M+ | 3.5–5.5% | Economies of scale reduce percentage |

**Floyd Hill example:** 4.75% (large project = lower end of range)
**Vail Pass example:** 5.25% (smaller project = mid-range)

## What Evaluators Look For in Price Proposals

### Competitive but Credible
- Fees should be reasonable for the scope — not artificially low
- An unrealistically low fee suggests the contractor doesn't understand the preconstruction workload or plans to cut corners

### Consistency with Technical Proposal
- The fee should support the staffing plan described in the technical proposal
- If the technical proposal promises 100% dedication of the PM, the hours and rates should reflect that

### Clear Assumptions
- State what's included and excluded
- Clarify duration assumptions
- Specify the number of OPCC deliverables and design review milestones

### No Hidden Costs
- Management fee percentage should clearly define inclusions/exclusions
- Evaluators will flag vague language that could lead to disputes later

---

## Important: The CAP Comes Later

The price proposal is NOT the construction bid. Key differences:

| Aspect | Price Proposal (at selection) | CAP (at ~90% design) |
|--------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| When | During procurement | After selection, during preconstruction |
| What | Advisory fees + markup % | Full construction price |
| How determined | Proposed by contractor | Negotiated (open-book) |
| Binding? | Yes (for precon and fee %) | Yes (becomes GMP) |
| What if no agreement? | N/A | CDOT can terminate and rebid |

The CM/GC Management Fee Percentage proposed at selection carries forward into the CAP — it becomes the markup applied to all direct construction costs when the full price is negotiated.
