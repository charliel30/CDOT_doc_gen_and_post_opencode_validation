# CM/GC Proposal Template — Overall Structure

This template shows the expected structure and layout of a CM/GC proposal submitted in response to a CDOT Request for Proposals. OpenCode should use this as the format guide when generating the final `cmgc_proposal.md`.

---

## Document Organization

A CM/GC proposal is submitted in **two sealed envelopes**. The final `cmgc_proposal.md` combines both into a single document with clear section breaks.

---

## ENVELOPE 1: TECHNICAL PROPOSAL

### Cover Letter (1 page)
- Addressed to CDOT's Selection Panel
- States the firm's intent to propose
- Identifies the project by name and number
- Names the authorized representative and contact information
- Signed by an officer authorized to bind the firm

---

### Section 1: Executive Summary (2–3 pages)
- Brief overview of the firm and why it is uniquely qualified for this project
- Summary of the proposed approach in 2–3 paragraphs
- Highlight of 2–3 key differentiators or innovations
- Statement of commitment to CDOT's project goals

---

### Section 2: Key Personnel and Organization (5–8 pages)

#### 2.1 Organizational Chart (1 page)
- Visual diagram showing the project team structure
- All key personnel named with titles and roles
- Reporting relationships clearly shown
- Subcontractor relationships indicated
- CDOT interface points identified

#### 2.2 Key Personnel Resumes (1 page per person)
For each key position, provide:
- Name and proposed role
- Years of experience (total and in CM/GC specifically)
- Relevant project experience (3–5 projects with owner, scope, value, and role)
- Professional certifications and education
- Percentage of time dedicated to this project

**Minimum key positions to address:**
- Project Manager / Construction Manager
- Project Superintendent
- Estimating Manager
- Scheduling Manager
- Safety Manager
- Quality Manager
- Environmental Compliance Manager

#### 2.3 Team Availability and Commitment
- Statement that key personnel are available and committed for the project duration
- Disclosure of other project commitments
- Plan for backfill if personnel changes are necessary

---

### Section 3: Project Understanding (3–5 pages)
- Demonstrates deep understanding of the project scope, goals, and constraints
- Identifies the key challenges specific to this project (not generic)
- Shows awareness of stakeholder concerns and community impacts
- References the environmental constraints and mitigation requirements
- Acknowledges the project's funding structure and budget pressures
- Demonstrates knowledge of the project corridor and site conditions

---

### Section 4: Project Approach (5–8 pages)

#### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach
- How the CM will collaborate with CDOT's design team
- Constructability review process and deliverables
- Cost estimating methodology and tools
- Value engineering approach
- Communication and reporting plan during preconstruction

#### 4.2 Construction Approach
- Proposed means and methods for major work elements
- Phasing strategy and sequencing
- Traffic management approach during construction
- Approach to working in the active I-70 corridor
- Equipment and resource mobilization plan

#### 4.3 Subcontractor Management
- Plan for identifying, selecting, and managing subcontractors
- Approach to meeting self-performance requirements (minimum 30% construction)
- DBE outreach and participation plan

---

### Section 5: Schedule (3–5 pages)

#### 5.1 Preconstruction Schedule
- Timeline for constructability reviews at each design milestone
- Cost estimate delivery dates aligned with design completion (30%, 60%, 90%)
- CAP negotiation timeline

#### 5.2 Construction Schedule
- Preliminary construction schedule (Gantt chart or CPM summary)
- Major milestones aligned with CDOT's targets
- Phasing plan showing geographic sections (East, Central, West for Floyd Hill)
- Seasonal constraints identified (winter weather, peak traffic periods)
- Critical path activities highlighted

#### 5.3 Schedule Risk and Contingency
- Key schedule risks and proposed float/contingency
- Approach to schedule recovery if delays occur
- Weather day assumptions

---

### Section 6: Risk Management (3–4 pages)

#### 6.1 Risk Identification
- Table of identified risks organized by category:
  - Geotechnical / subsurface
  - Environmental / permitting
  - Traffic / public safety
  - Schedule / weather
  - Cost / market conditions
  - Design / constructability
  - Utility conflicts
  - Stakeholder / community

#### 6.2 Risk Mitigation Strategies
- For each major risk: likelihood, impact, and specific mitigation actions
- How risks will be tracked and communicated to CDOT
- Risk register approach during preconstruction and construction

---

### Section 7: Safety Plan (2–3 pages)
- Firm's safety record (EMR, OSHA incident rates for past 3 years)
- Project-specific safety hazards and mitigation
- Safety training and certification requirements for all workers
- Safety culture initiatives (toolbox talks, near-miss reporting, stop-work authority)
- Public safety plan for active traffic zones
- Emergency response procedures

---

### Section 8: Quality Management (2–3 pages)
- Quality Assurance / Quality Control (QA/QC) organizational structure
- Inspection and testing protocols for major work items
- Documentation and reporting procedures
- Non-conformance identification and resolution process
- Materials testing and acceptance plan

---

### Section 9: Innovation and Value Engineering (1–2 pages)
- Specific ideas for cost savings, schedule acceleration, or quality improvement
- Technologies or methods proposed (e.g., BIM, drone surveying, advanced formwork)
- Past examples of successful innovation on similar projects
- NOTE: Ideas should be concrete and project-specific, not generic statements

---

## ENVELOPE 2: PRICE PROPOSAL

### Section 10: Price Proposal (2–4 pages)

#### 10.1 Preconstruction Services Fee
- Lump sum fee for CM services during the preconstruction/design phase
- Breakdown by task category (constructability reviews, estimating, scheduling, risk analysis)
- Fee schedule for key personnel (hourly rates and estimated hours)

#### 10.2 CM/GC Management Fee Percentage
- The contractor's proposed markup percentage on construction costs
- This fee covers the contractor's home office overhead and profit during construction
- Applied to all direct construction costs including subcontractor work

#### 10.3 Fee Assumptions
- What is included in and excluded from the proposed fees
- Assumptions about preconstruction duration
- Assumptions about the number of design milestones / OPCC deliverables

---

## APPENDICES (not counted toward page limit)

- Appendix A: Contractor prequalification confirmation
- Appendix B: Bonding capacity letter from surety
- Appendix C: Insurance certificates
- Appendix D: DBE participation commitment
- Appendix E: Relevant project experience detail sheets
- Appendix F: Safety record documentation (EMR, OSHA 300 logs)

---

## Formatting Requirements

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Page limit (Technical) | 30–50 pages (per RFP; appendices excluded) |
| Font | 12-point Times New Roman or Arial |
| Charts/tables font | 10-point minimum |
| Spacing | Single-spaced |
| Margins | 1 inch on all sides |
| Page numbering | Sequential, bottom center |
| Headers | Section titles on each page |
| File format | PDF (electronic submission) |
