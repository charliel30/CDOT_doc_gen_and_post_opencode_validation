## Section 1: Executive Summary

Kraemer North America, LLC is a heavy civil construction firm with 100+ years of experience building highway infrastructure in challenging environments. We have delivered multiple CDOT projects in the I-70 Mountain Corridor, including the I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion (~$70M) and the I-25 North Express Lanes (~$700M+).

Our approach to the Floyd Hill project centers on three priorities: (1) early constructability input on the segmental bridge and rock excavation designs to reduce cost and schedule risk, (2) a phasing strategy that keeps I-70 moving during peak travel periods while maximizing productive construction windows, and (3) environmental stewardship that protects Clear Creek water quality and wildlife corridors throughout construction.

We propose two innovations that can deliver significant value: an accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork that reduces the bridge schedule by approximately 4 months, and a real-time water quality monitoring system with automated sediment control that exceeds SWEEP ITF requirements.

Kraemer North America is fully committed to CDOT's goal of completing westbound I-70 in its new alignment by end of 2027 and full project completion in 2029.

## Section 3: Project Understanding

The I-70 Floyd Hill project addresses one of Colorado's most critical transportation bottlenecks — an 8-mile stretch of I-70 through Clear Creek County where westbound traffic from Denver climbs nearly 2,000 feet to Floyd Hill then descends 600 feet in less than 2 miles to the I-70/US 6 interchange.

We have identified the following key challenges that will drive our approach:

**1. Segmental Bridge Construction Over Live Traffic**
The project includes over 10,000 feet of new bridges, with the largest exceeding one mile in length. A new 115-foot tall bridge will carry westbound traffic in a shifted alignment above Clear Creek. Cast-in-place segmental construction with balanced cantilever methods will require precise execution over active I-70 traffic lanes.

**2. Massive Rock Excavation in Constrained Corridor**
Removing hundreds of thousands of tons of rock from hillsides above I-70 requires controlled blasting in close proximity to live traffic, existing structures, Clear Creek, and residential areas.

**3. Clear Creek Environmental Protections**
Clear Creek runs through the entire 8-mile corridor. The FONSI and 1041 permit impose strict requirements for water quality protection, sediment control, and creek/riparian restoration.

**4. Wildlife Connectivity**
Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire, 2 miles of deer fencing, 7 escape ramps, and a deer guard on CR 65 must be constructed while maintaining wildlife movement during the multi-year construction period.

**5. Three-Section Concurrent Construction**
The East (July 2023–Summer 2026), Central (Fall 2024–Late 2028), and West (Spring 2024–Summer 2028) sections will be under construction simultaneously.

**6. Traffic Management on Colorado's Primary Mountain Corridor**
I-70 is the only east-west interstate through the Colorado mountains. Weekend ski traffic, summer recreation traffic, and local Idaho Springs/Central City traffic must be accommodated throughout construction.

**7. Multi-Stakeholder Coordination**
Clear Creek County (1041 permit authority), Idaho Springs, FHWA, the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association, Colorado Parks and Wildlife, and the I-70 Coalition all have active interests in this project.

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

**Collaborative Design Integration**
We will co-locate a preconstruction team at CDOT's project office to provide daily interaction with the AtkinsRealis design team. Weekly constructability review meetings will be supplemented by real-time informal consultation.

**Constructability Review Process**
At each design milestone (30%, 60%, 90%), we will deliver a formal Constructability Review Report identifying:
- Construction sequencing conflicts
- Access and staging challenges
- Opportunities to simplify construction without compromising design intent
- Material or method substitutions that reduce cost or schedule

**Cost Estimating**
Lisa Nguyen will develop OPCCs at 30%, 60%, and 90% design using our proprietary estimating database calibrated to Colorado mountain construction.

**Value Engineering**
We will present formal VE proposals at the 30% and 60% milestones, with each proposal including a cost-benefit analysis and constructability assessment.

### 4.2 Construction Approach

**Segmental Bridge Construction**
We propose cast-in-place segmental construction using a traveling formwork system with a 7-day casting cycle per segment. Our team has completed 4 segmental bridge projects in mountain terrain.

**Rock Excavation and Blasting**
Jeff Carpenter has supervised over 200 controlled blasts on mountain highway projects. Our approach:
- Pre-blast geotechnical surveys (core samples every 50 feet)
- Electronic detonator systems for precise blast timing
- Blast monitoring stations at 100-foot intervals
- All blasting during weekday off-peak hours
