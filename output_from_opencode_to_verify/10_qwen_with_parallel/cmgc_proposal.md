## Project Purpose and Significance

The I-70 Floyd Hill to Veterans Memorial Tunnels project addresses critical safety, capacity, and reliability concerns in one of Colorado's most challenging mountain corridors. Westbound vehicles climbing from Denver gain nearly 2,000 feet of elevation to the top of Floyd Hill, then descend 600 feet in less than 2 miles to the I-70/US 6 interchange at Clear Creek. This steep grade, combined with tight curves (existing bridges rated for only 45 mph) and heavy ski/recreation traffic, creates one of the worst recurring bottlenecks on I-70. The project's primary goal is to improve safety and reduce congestion on this critical transportation artery that serves both local and regional travelers.

## Key Challenges Identified

Based on the project specifications and environmental constraints, the following key challenges have been identified:

1. **Steep Grades and Elevation Changes**: The project involves a 7% profile grade on the east section, creating significant drainage engineering challenges. The westbound climb of nearly 2,000 feet followed by a 600-foot descent in less than 2 miles presents unique construction and safety considerations.

2. **Large Volume Rock Excavation**: Extensive rock scaling and blasting operations are required throughout the corridor. The project involves over 600,000 tons of material removal, with 135+ blasts planned and nearly 40,000+ tons removed from the central/west sections as of late 2024.

3. **Segmental Bridge Construction Over Live Traffic**: The project includes over 10,000 feet of new bridges, with the largest single bridge being nearly 2 miles long. Construction of these structures over live I-70 traffic presents significant safety and traffic management challenges.

4. **Environmental Protection Requirements**: The project corridor traverses Clear Creek, requiring extensive environmental protection measures. Key concerns include sediment runoff into Clear Creek, chloride contamination from winter maintenance, and protection of wildlife movement corridors.

5. **Construction Phasing Across Three Geographic Sections**: Construction is being executed in three geographic sections simultaneously (East, Central, and West), each with different start dates and completion targets, requiring complex coordination.

6. **Weekend and Seasonal Traffic Surges**: The project must accommodate peak ski traffic and weekend traffic patterns, with construction activities potentially affecting these high-usage periods.

7. **Stakeholder Complexity**: Multiple stakeholders are involved, including CDOT, FHWA, Clear Creek County, Idaho Springs, Colorado SHPO, Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association, SWEEP ITF, Colorado Parks and Wildlife, and the I-70 Coalition.

These challenges require careful consideration in our approach to ensure successful project delivery while maintaining safety, environmental compliance, and stakeholder satisfaction.# Section 4: Project Approach

## Construction Approach

### Major Scope Elements

**Bridge Construction**: For segmental bridge structures, we will utilize our proven balanced cantilever construction method with cast-in-place segments. This approach has been successfully implemented on similar projects including I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion. For spliced concrete girders, we will apply our standard construction techniques with careful attention to construction sequencing and traffic management.

**Roadway Realignment**: Our approach to roadway realignment will incorporate advanced surveying techniques and detailed geotechnical analysis to ensure proper alignment and drainage. We will utilize our experience with steep-grade construction projects in the I-70 corridor.

**Retaining Walls**: We will employ our standard shotcrete application methods for retaining walls, which have been successfully used in other mountain corridor projects. The natural-looking finish requirements will be met through careful material selection and application techniques.

**Wildlife Crossings**: Our approach will ensure compliance with Colorado Parks and Wildlife standards and CDOT specifications, incorporating the required deer fencing, escape ramps, and underpasses.

### Phasing Strategy

We propose a phased approach that aligns with the project's geographic sections:

- **East Section**: Construction begins July 2023, with emphasis on early completion of major bridge elements to minimize traffic impacts
- **Central Section**: Begins Fall 2024, with careful coordination of rock blasting and bridge construction activities
- **West Section**: Begins Spring 2024, with attention to the challenging alignment changes and wildlife crossing requirements

### Traffic Management

Our traffic management approach addresses both weekday and weekend/ski traffic patterns:

- **Weekend Closure Restrictions**: No closures during ski season (Friday PM to Sunday PM) for critical work areas
- **Nightwork for Critical Operations**: Critical activities such as rock blasting will be scheduled for off-peak hours to minimize impact on traffic
- **Detour Planning**: Comprehensive detour plans have been developed to accommodate traffic during major work activities

### Rock Blasting Operations

Our rock blasting approach will follow proven protocols:

- **Blast Sequencing**: Careful planning to minimize disruption to I-70 traffic and surrounding communities
