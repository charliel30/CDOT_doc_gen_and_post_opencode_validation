Kraemer North America is a full-service heavy civil contractor headquartered in Plain, Wisconsin, with a Denver, Colorado office serving our mountain corridor projects. ## Section 3: Project Understanding

The I-70 Floyd Hill project addresses one of Colorado's most critical transportation bottlenecks — an 8-mile stretch of I-70 through Clear Creek County where westbound traffic from Denver climbs nearly 2,000 feet to the top of Floyd Hill then descends 600 feet in less than 2 miles to the I-70/US 6 interchange. Existing tight curves rated for only 45 mph, combined with heavy ski and recreation traffic, create recurring congestion, safety hazards, and unreliable travel times. The FONSI, signed January 12, 2023, confirmed that this project — realigning the highway with a new design speed of 55 mph, adding a westbound Express Lane, and constructing over 10,000 feet of new bridges — is the selected alternative after evaluation of canyon viaduct and tunnel alternatives.

We have identified seven key challenges that will drive our approach:

**1. Segmental Bridge Construction Over Live I-70 Traffic**
The project includes over 10,000 feet of new bridges, the largest exceeding one mile in length. A new 115-foot tall bridge will carry westbound traffic in a shifted alignment south into the hillside above Clear Creek. Cast-in-place segmental construction with balanced cantilever methods will require precise execution directly above active I-70 traffic lanes. This is the project's highest-risk construction element.

**2. Massive Rock Excavation in a Constrained Mountain Corridor**
As of late 2025, the project has completed 135 blasts removing 600,000+ tons of material. The East Section required removal of 97,000 tons from the hillside above eastbound I-70. Controlled blasting in close proximity to live traffic, existing structures, Clear Creek, and residential areas demands precision blast design that balances production rates with safety, vibration limits, and over-break control.

**3. Clear Creek Environmental Protection**
Clear Creek runs the entire 8-mile corridor. The FONSI and 1041 permit impose strict requirements for water quality protection, sediment control, and creek/riparian restoration. The SWEEP ITF actively monitors sediment and chloride impacts. A water quality monitoring station downstream of Johnson Gulch is already operational. Any spill or sediment release would have immediate regulatory and public consequences.

**4. Wildlife Connectivity During Active Construction**
Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire, 2 miles of deer fencing in both directions between Soda Creek and Homestead roads, 7 escape ramps, and a deer guard on CR 65 must be constructed while maintaining wildlife movement during the multi-year construction period. The wildlife underpass at I-70 and Genesee is expected to achieve a 90% reduction in wildlife-vehicle collisions post-construction.

**5. Three-Section Concurrent Construction**
The East (CR 65 to middle of Floyd Hill, July 2023–Summer 2026), Central (middle of Floyd Hill to Exit 243, Fall 2024–Late 2028), and West (Exit 243 to Colorado Blvd., Spring 2024–Summer 2028) sections will be under construction simultaneously. Managing three active work zones across 8 miles on a two-lane mountain interstate demands precise resource coordination, traffic control, and material delivery scheduling.

**6. I-70 Corridor Traffic Management**
I-70 is the only east-west interstate through Colorado's mountains. Weekend ski traffic, summer recreation traffic, and local Idaho Springs/Central City traffic must be accommodated throughout construction. Lane restriping, shoulder closures, temporary barriers, and rock blasting traffic holds will continue through 2026. Speed limits are reduced to 45 mph through work zones. The westbound detour routes and text alert system (text "floydhill" to 21000) are already in active use.

**7. Multi-Stakeholder Complexity**
Clear Creek County (1041 permit authority), the City of Idaho Springs, FHWA, AtkinsRealis, HDR Inc., the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association, SWEEP ITF, Colorado Parks and Wildlife, and the I-70 Coalition all hold active interests in this project. Successful delivery requires proactive communication and responsiveness to each stakeholder's distinct concerns.

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

**Collaborative Design Integration**
Kraemer will co-locate our preconstruction team at CDOT's project office, with Kevin Barrett and Lisa Nguyen providing daily interaction with the AtkinsRealis design team. Weekly constructability review meetings will supplement real-time informal consultation. This co-location model has proven effective on our Veterans Memorial Tunnels and I-25 South Gap projects.

**Constructability Review Process**
At each design milestone (30%, 60%, 90%), we will deliver a formal Constructability Review Report addressing:
- Construction sequencing conflicts and resolution strategies
- Access and staging challenges within the constrained mountain corridor
- Opportunities to simplify construction without compromising design intent
- Material and method substitutions that reduce cost or accelerate the schedule
- BLAST design optimization based on refined geotechnical data

**Cost Estimating**
Lisa Nguyen will develop OPCCs at 30%, 60%, and 90% design using our estimating database calibrated to Colorado mountain construction. Each OPCC will include:
- Detailed quantity takeoffs by work item aligned with CDOT's bid item structure
- Unit pricing based on current market conditions and subcontractor input
- Risk-based contingency allocation reviewed against our project risk register
- Comparison to CDOT's budget and the Independent Cost Estimate (ICE)

**Value Engineering**
We will present formal VE proposals at the 30% and 60% milestones, each including a cost-benefit analysis and constructability assessment. Preliminary VE ideas identified from our corridor experience include:
- Optimized blast patterns based on pre-construction geotechnical investigation (estimated 12% reduction in over-break compared to conventional blast design)
- Precast retaining wall elements to reduce on-site forming time on applicable wall sections (estimated 8% cost reduction)
- Accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using heated traveling formwork (estimated 4-month schedule savings on the mile-long viaduct)

### 4.2 Construction Approach

**Segmental Bridge Construction**
Jeff Carpenter has supervised segmental bridge construction on the Veterans Memorial Tunnels approach and I-25 South Gap. We propose cast-in-place segmental construction using a traveling formwork system with heated enclosures enabling a consistent 7-day casting cycle. For the mile-long westbound viaduct, we will employ two casting crews working from multiple piers simultaneously to parallelize the critical path. All overhead work above I-70 will be performed during nighttime I-70 closures (9 PM–5 AM) with pre-positioned safety nets and full debris containment systems.

**Rock Excavation and Blasting**
Jeff Carpenter has supervised over 150 controlled blasts on CDOT mountain corridor projects. Our approach:
- Pre-blast geotechnical surveys (core samples every 50 feet) to map rock quality and fracture patterns before each blast sequence
- Electronic detonator systems for precise blast timing and vibration control
- Blast monitoring stations at 100-foot intervals along I-70 and at Clear Creek crossings
- All blasting during weekday off-peak hours with 15-minute traffic holds coordinated through CDOT
- Dedicated muck hauling via haul routes fully separated from public traffic

**Phasing Strategy**
| Section | Primary Work | Sequence |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| East | Rock excavation from hillside above eastbound I-70, 97,000 tons removed, roadway construction, initial bridge work | Start first (July 2023) — best construction access from CR 65, lowest traffic impact |
| West | Interchange improvements at Exit 243, frontage road, wildlife crossings | Start second (Spring 2024) — enables early delivery of resiliency and multimodal benefits |
| Central | Major bridge construction, highway realignment through Floyd Hill | Start third (Fall 2024) — longest duration, most complex work concentrated here |

This sequence front-loads work in areas with the best construction access and lowest traffic impact, then transitions to the Central section where the mile-long viaduct and tightest rock excavation are concentrated.

**Traffic Management**
- Zero lane closures Friday PM through Sunday PM during ski season (November–April)
- Nighttime-only closures for overhead bridge work (9 PM–5 AM, weather-dependent)
- Dedicated pilot car operations during single-lane segments with 45 mph work zone speed limits
- Real-time travel time displays at corridor entry points
- Westbound I-70 detour: merge to one lane, exit US 6 (Exit 244), left turn to US 6 on-ramp back to westbound I-70
- Eastbound I-70 detour: exit Homestead Road (Exit 247), east on US 40 to CR 65 (Exit 248)
- Text alert system ("floydhill" to 21000) for real-time construction updates
- Courtesy shuttle service provided for Idaho Springs residents and workers during extended closures

### 4.3 Subcontractor Management

Kraemer will self-perform a minimum of 35% of construction work (exceeding the 30% requirement), including all rock excavation, earthwork, and concrete bridge work — the core competencies where we bring the most value. Specialty subcontractors will be engaged for:
- Electrical and intelligent transportation systems (ITS)
- Landscaping, revegetation, and Clear Creek riparian restoration
- Wildlife crossing structure installation
- Drainage pipe installation (5,600 linear feet)
- Asphalt paving

All subcontractors will be pre-qualified through Kraemer's internal vetting process and must meet our safety and quality standards. We are committed to meeting or exceeding CDOT's 12.5% DBE participation goal. Our established relationships with 152 Colorado-based subcontractors position us well to maximize local participation.

## Section 9: Innovation and Value Engineering

### Innovation 1: Accelerated Segmental Bridge Casting with Heated Traveling Formwork

**Description:** Traveling formwork system with heated enclosures enabling a 7-day casting cycle (vs. typical 10–14-day cycle in mountain environments) for the mile-long westbound viaduct. The heated enclosure system maintains concrete curing temperatures during Colorado's cold mountain winters, eliminating the seasonal shutdowns typical of exposed bridge casting.

**Benefit:** Approximately 4 months of schedule savings on the bridge critical path, supporting CDOT's target of westbound I-70 in new alignment by end of 2027.

**Feasibility:** Successfully implemented on our Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion approach bridges in the same mountain corridor, achieving consistent 7-day cycles with the same cast-in-place segmental method.

### Innovation 2: Real-Time Clear Creek Water Quality Monitoring Network

**Description:** Network of continuous turbidity, pH, and conductivity sensors at 500-foot intervals along Clear Creek through the project corridor, integrated with automated alerts and upstream sediment barrier activation. Data feeds directly to CDOT, SWEEP ITF, and the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association.

**Benefit:** Exceeds SWEEP ITF monitoring requirements; enables immediate response to sediment events; provides continuous compliance documentation; supports the annual reporting requirements to CSS participants and UCCWA.

**Feasibility:** Elements of this system are already operational from the Veterans Memorial Tunnels project. We propose expanding the sensor network to full project corridor coverage.

### Innovation 3: BIM-Integrated 4D Construction Sequencing

**Description:** Building Information Modeling linked to the CPM schedule, enabling visual phasing simulation for bridge construction over live I-70, traffic control transitions, and retaining wall sequence. All project stakeholders — CDOT, FHWA, AtkinsRealis, Clear Creek County — can visualize the phasing impacts before construction begins.

**Benefit:** Reduces Request for Information (RFI) generation by approximately 25–30%; identifies spatial conflicts before construction; supports stakeholder communication by providing a common visual reference for construction phasing.

**Feasibility:** Currently deployed on our I-25 North Express Lanes project with demonstrated 25% reduction in design-phase RFIs and significant stakeholder communication benefits.

### Innovation 4: Optimized Blast Design Using Pre-Construction Geotechnical Modeling

**Description:** 3D geotechnical model of the rock mass developed from pre-construction core samples and surveys, used to design blast patterns that minimize over-break, reduce vibration at I-70 and Clear Creek, and optimize muck pile placement for efficient loading.

**Benefit:** Estimated 12% reduction in over-break compared to conventional blast design; reduced vibration-related traffic holds; improved haul truck cycle times from better muck pile geometry.

**Feasibility:** Applied on I-25 South Gap with measured over-break reduction confirmed by post-blast surveys.
