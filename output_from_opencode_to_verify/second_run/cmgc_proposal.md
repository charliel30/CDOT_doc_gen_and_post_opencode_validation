## Section 1: Executive Summary

Kraemer North America, LLC is a heavy civil construction firm headquartered in Plain, Wisconsin, with over 100 years of experience building highway, bridge, and marine infrastructure across the United States. Our Denver, Colorado office has been active in the I-70 Mountain Corridor for more than two decades, and we have delivered multiple large-scale CDOT projects that demonstrate our capabilities in complex, environmentally sensitive mountain terrain.

Our approach to the Floyd Hill project centers on three priorities: (1) early, continuous constructability input on the segmental bridge and rock excavation designs to reduce cost and schedule risk from the outset of preconstruction; (2) a phased construction strategy that keeps I-70 moving during peak travel periods while maximizing productive construction windows across all three geographic sections simultaneously; and (3) environmental stewardship that protects Clear Creek water quality and wildlife corridors throughout every phase of construction.

We propose two innovations that can deliver measurable value on this project: an accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork with heated enclosures — a technique we have refined through our Veterans Memorial Tunnels and I-25 South Gap work in the same corridor — and a real-time water quality monitoring network with automated sediment barrier activation that exceeds SWEEP ITF requirements and provides continuous compliance documentation. These are proven techniques from our recent Colorado mountain corridor projects, not untested concepts.

Kraemer North America is fully committed to CDOT's goal of completing westbound I-70 in its new alignment by end of 2027 and full project completion in 2029, within the adjusted $905M budget.

## Section 3: Project Understanding

The I-70 Floyd Hill project addresses one of Colorado's most critical transportation bottlenecks — an 8-mile stretch of I-70 through Clear Creek County where westbound traffic from Denver climbs nearly 2,000 feet of elevation to the top of Floyd Hill then descends 600 feet in less than 2 miles to the I-70/US 6 interchange at Clear Creek. Existing bridges are rated for only 45 mph due to tight horizontal curves, and heavy ski and recreation traffic combine with steep grades to create recurring congestion, safety hazards, and unreliable travel times that impact the state's economy and quality of life.

The project spans from County Road 65 (Exit 248, west of Evergreen) to Colorado Boulevard (Exit 241, eastern Idaho Springs) on I-70 Mile Points 241 to 248. The $700M preliminary estimate (2020) was adjusted to $905M (2025) reflecting current market conditions. Funding includes a $100M federal INFRA grant awarded September 2022 under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, state funding under SB 21-260, and borrowing against future Express Lane toll revenue.

We have identified the following key challenges that will drive our project approach:

**1. Segmental Bridge Construction Over Live Traffic**
The project includes over 10,000 feet of new bridge structures, with the largest exceeding one mile in length. A new 115-foot tall bridge will carry westbound traffic in a shifted alignment south into the hillside above Clear Creek. Cast-in-place segmental construction with balanced cantilever methods will require precise execution over active I-70 traffic lanes. This is the project's highest-risk construction element, and successful delivery requires early design input on both the bridge geometry and the construction sequence.

**2. Massive Rock Excavation in a Constrained Mountain Corridor**
Removing more than 600,000 tons of rock from hillsides above I-70 requires controlled blasting in close proximity to live traffic, existing structures, Clear Creek, and residential areas. Blast design must balance production rates with safety and vibration limits for surrounding structures and the creek. The 7% profile grade on the east section adds drainage and access complexity during excavation. As of late 2025, 135 blasts have removed 600,000+ tons of material, demonstrating the scale of this work.

**3. Clear Creek Environmental Protections**
Clear Creek runs through the entire 8-mile project corridor. The FONSI (signed January 12, 2023) and the Clear Creek County 1041 permit impose strict requirements for water quality protection, sediment control, and creek/riparian restoration. A dedicated water quality monitoring station has been installed downstream of Johnson Gulch. The SWEEP ITF actively monitors sediment and chloride impacts, and the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association requires annual monitoring reports. Any spill or sediment release into Clear Creek would have immediate regulatory and public relations consequences.

**4. Wildlife Connectivity**
Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire, 2 miles of deer fencing in both directions between Soda Creek and Homestead roads, 7 escape ramps, and 1 deer guard on County Road 65 must be constructed while maintaining wildlife movement during the multi-year construction period. The wildlife underpass at I-70 and Genesee is expected to achieve a 90% reduction in wildlife-vehicle collisions. Seasonal construction restrictions near wildlife movement corridors add scheduling complexity.

**5. Three-Section Concurrent Construction**
The East (CR 65 to middle of Floyd Hill, July 2023–Summer 2026), West (Exit 243 to Exit 241, Spring 2024–Summer 2028), and Central (middle of Floyd Hill to Exit 243, Fall 2024–Late 2028) sections will be under construction simultaneously. Managing three active work zones in an 8-mile corridor on a two-lane mountain interstate requires precise coordination of resources, traffic control, and material deliveries.

**6. Traffic Management on Colorado's Primary Mountain Corridor**
I-70 is the only east-west interstate through the Colorado mountains. Weekend ski traffic, summer recreation traffic, and local Idaho Springs/Central City traffic must be accommodated throughout construction. The traveling public expects real-time information, minimal full closures, nighttime-only work when possible, and safe, well-signed work zones. Speed limits are reduced to 45 mph through work zones, and lane restriping, shoulder closures, and temporary barriers are in place throughout construction. Text alerts are available by texting "floydhill" to 21000. As of September 2024, a courtesy shuttle service had provided 650 trips for Idaho Springs residents during extended closures.

**7. Multi-Stakeholder Coordination**
The project involves Clear Creek County (1041 permit authority), the City of Idaho Springs (local businesses and residents), FHWA (NEPA signatory and federal oversight), the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association (water quality), the SWEEP ITF (sediment and erosion), Colorado Parks and Wildlife (wildlife crossings), the I-70 Coalition (corridor advocacy), and Colorado SHPO (historic preservation, including a treatment plan for an identified archaeological site). Successful delivery requires proactive communication and responsiveness to each stakeholder's concerns.

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

**Collaborative Design Integration**
We will co-locate a preconstruction team at CDOT's project office, including our Estimating Manager and a constructability lead, to provide daily interaction with the AtkinsRealis design team. This mirrors the approach that enabled us to optimize bridge design and identify rock excavation efficiencies before the CAP was set on the Veterans Memorial Tunnels project and the I-25 South Gap project.

**Constructability Review Process**
At each design milestone (30%, 60%, 90%), we will deliver a formal Constructability Review Report identifying:
- Construction sequencing conflicts
- Access and staging challenges
- Opportunities to simplify construction without compromising design intent
- Material or method substitutions that reduce cost or schedule
- Bridge design conflicts with actual geotechnical conditions identified during preconstruction investigation

**Cost Estimating**
Our Estimating Manager will develop OPCCs at 30%, 60%, and 90% design using our Colorado mountain construction database calibrated to current market conditions. Each OPCC will include:
- Detailed quantity takeoffs by work item
- Unit pricing based on current market conditions and subcontractor input
- Risk-based contingency allocation
- Comparison to CDOT's budget and the ICE prepared by the Independent Cost Estimator

**Value Engineering**
We will present formal VE proposals at the 30% and 60% milestones, with each proposal including a cost-benefit analysis and constructability assessment. Based on our experience on this corridor, preliminary VE ideas include:
- Accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork (estimated 4-month schedule savings on the mile-long westbound bridge)
- Precast retaining wall elements to reduce on-site forming time (estimated 8% cost reduction on applicable walls)
- Optimized blast patterns based on pre-construction geotechnical investigation (estimated reduction in over-break quantities)

**Open-Book Pricing**
We are fully committed to open-book pricing throughout preconstruction. CDOT will have full visibility into all cost components — subcontractor quotes, material takeoffs, labor estimates, equipment costs, and overhead calculations — consistent with CM/GC best practices and FHWA requirements.

### 4.2 Construction Approach

**Segmental Bridge Construction**
We propose cast-in-place segmental construction using a traveling formwork system. For the mile-long westbound bridge over Clear Creek, we will deploy two casting crews working from multiple piers simultaneously to parallelize the critical path. Overhead bridge work over live I-70 traffic will be performed during nighttime I-70 closures (9 PM–5 AM) with pre-positioned safety nets and debris containment systems. These techniques were successfully employed on our Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion and I-25 South Gap projects.

**Rock Excavation and Blasting**
We will implement the following approach for controlled blasting adjacent to I-70:
- Pre-blast geotechnical surveys (core samples) to map rock quality and fracture patterns before each major blast sequence
- Electronic detonator systems for precise blast timing and vibration control
- Blast monitoring stations at defined intervals along I-70 and at Clear Creek
- All blasting during weekday off-peak hours with traffic holds
- Muck hauling via dedicated haul routes separated from public traffic
- Post-blast inspections before reopening lanes to traffic

As of late 2025, our team had completed 135+ blasts removing 600,000+ tons of material while maintaining I-70 mobility.

**Phasing Strategy**
| Section | Primary Work | Sequence |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| East | Rock excavation, roadway construction, initial bridge work | Start first (July 2023) — most construction access, least traffic impact |
| West | Interchange improvements, frontage road, wildlife crossings | Start second (Spring 2024) — enables early delivery of resiliency benefits |
| Central | Major bridge construction, highway realignment | Start third (Fall 2024) — requires longest duration, most complex work |

This sequence front-loads work in areas where construction access is best and traffic impacts are lowest, then transitions to the Central section where the major bridge work is concentrated.

**Traffic Management**
- Zero lane closures on Friday PM through Sunday PM during ski season (November–April)
- Nighttime-only closures for overhead bridge work (9 PM–5 AM, weather-dependent)
- Dedicated pilot car operations during single-lane segments
- Real-time travel time displays at corridor entry points
- Coordination with CDOT's text alert system (text "floydhill" to 21000)
- Courtesy shuttle service for Idaho Springs residents during extended closures
- Westbound I-70 detour during bridge work: merge to one lane, exit US 6 (Exit 244), left turn to US 6 on-ramp back to westbound I-70
- Eastbound I-70 detour: exit Homestead Road (Exit 247), east on US 40 to CR 65 (Exit 248)

### 4.3 Subcontractor Management

We will self-perform a minimum of 35% of construction work (exceeding the 30% requirement), including all rock excavation, earthwork, and concrete bridge work. Our core competencies in bridge and earthwork are typically self-performed, consistent with our track record on the I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion and I-25 South Gap.

Specialty subcontractors will be engaged for:
- Electrical and ITS systems
- Landscaping and revegetation
- Wildlife crossing structure specialized work
- Drainage pipe installation
- Asphalt paving

On the Floyd Hill project as delivered, 162 subcontractors have been engaged, of which 152 are Colorado-based businesses, demonstrating our commitment to local contractor participation.

We are committed to meeting or exceeding CDOT's 12.5% DBE participation goal for this project. We will conduct active DBE outreach and have established mentoring partnerships with emerging DBE firms.

## Section 9: Innovation and Value Engineering

### Innovation 1: Accelerated Segmental Bridge Casting with Traveling Formwork

**Description:** Traveling formwork system with heated enclosures enabling accelerated casting cycles for segmental bridge construction over Clear Creek.

**Benefit:** Estimated 4-month schedule savings on the mile-long westbound bridge.

**Feasibility:** This technique has been refined through our Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion and I-25 South Gap projects in the same I-70 Mountain Corridor. The 115-foot tall segmental structures on this project are directly comparable to work we have successfully completed.

### Innovation 2: Real-Time Water Quality Monitoring Network

**Description:** Network of continuous turbidity, pH, and conductivity sensors along Clear Creek with automated alerts and upstream sediment barrier activation.

**Benefit:** Exceeds SWEEP ITF monitoring requirements; enables immediate response to sediment events; provides continuous compliance documentation for UCCWA annual reporting.

**Feasibility:** This approach directly addresses the water quality monitoring requirements identified in the FONSI and stakeholder requirements. The monitoring station downstream of Johnson Gulch provides an established baseline for sensor deployment.

### Innovation 3: BIM-Integrated 4D Construction Sequencing

**Description:** Building Information Modeling linked to the CPM schedule, enabling visual phasing simulation for bridge construction, traffic control, and utility conflicts.

**Benefit:** Reduces RFI generation; enables stakeholder visualization of phasing impacts; identifies spatial conflicts before construction.

**Feasibility:** BIM coordination is particularly valuable for the three-section concurrent construction approach, where spatial conflicts between East, West, and Central sections could otherwise cause delays.

### Innovation 4: Precast Retaining Wall System

**Description:** Factory-produced retaining wall panels transported and erected on-site for suitable locations, replacing conventional cast-in-place walls.

**Benefit:** Estimated 8% cost reduction on applicable walls; reduced on-site forming labor; improved quality control through factory conditions.

**Feasibility:** Structural analysis required during preconstruction to confirm applicability for Floyd Hill's geotechnical conditions. The nearly 4 miles of retaining walls represent a significant scope where this technique could apply.
