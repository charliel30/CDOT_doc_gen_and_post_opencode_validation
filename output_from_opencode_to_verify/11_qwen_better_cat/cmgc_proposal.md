## Section 1: Executive Summary

Kraemer North America is a Wisconsin-based heavy civil contractor with 100+ years of experience building highway infrastructure in the Rocky Mountain region. We have delivered multiple CDOT CM/GC projects, including the I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion, I-25 North Express Lanes, and I-25 South Gap projects, all involving steep mountain terrain, environmental sensitivity, and complex phasing.

Our approach to the Floyd Hill project centers on three priorities: **(1)** early constructability input on the segmental bridge and rock excavation designs to reduce cost and schedule risk, **(2)** a phasing strategy that keeps I-70 moving during peak travel periods while maximizing productive construction windows, and **(3)** environmental stewardship that protects Clear Creek water quality and wildlife corridors throughout construction.

We propose two innovations that can deliver significant value: an accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork that reduces the bridge schedule by approximately 4 months, and a real-time water quality monitoring system with automated sediment control that exceeds SWEEP ITF requirements. These are proven techniques from our recent mountain corridor projects.

Kraemer is fully committed to CDOT's goal of completing westbound I-70 in its new alignment by end of 2027 and full project completion in 2029.

## Section 3: Project Understanding

The I-70 Floyd Hill to Veterans Memorial Tunnels Improvements project represents one of the most challenging and important transportation projects in Colorado's mountain corridor. As a firm with extensive experience in this environment, we understand the project's complexities and constraints.

Key challenges specific to this project include:
- The steep 2,000-foot elevation climb followed by a 600-foot descent in a short distance creates unique engineering and construction challenges
- The requirement to add a third westbound lane through Floyd Hill while maintaining safety and traffic flow
- Extensive rock excavation and blasting operations in a sensitive environment adjacent to Clear Creek
- Complex traffic management during construction with ongoing I-70 operations
- Construction of over 10,000 feet of new bridges and nearly 4 miles of retaining walls
- Implementation of wildlife crossings and other environmental mitigation measures
- Significant stakeholder engagement requirements with CDOT, FHWA, Clear Creek County, and local communities

We acknowledge that the project's funding structure presents challenges, with a total budget of $905 million as of 2025. This budget must be managed carefully to ensure project completion while meeting quality, safety, and environmental standards.

Our team is familiar with the geographic conditions and site-specific challenges of the Floyd Hill corridor, including the extensive geotechnical work required with 600,000+ tons of material removed and 135+ blasts conducted.

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

Kraemer will collaborate with CDOT's design team through a structured constructability review process that begins at the 30% design milestone. Our approach includes:

- Conducting detailed constructability reviews at 30%, 60%, and 90% design milestones
- Providing cost estimating support aligned with each design milestone
- Supporting CAP negotiation with detailed cost breakdowns
- Engaging in value engineering discussions focused on schedule acceleration and cost optimization

We will provide a comprehensive communication plan that includes weekly status meetings, monthly progress reports, and quarterly management reviews with CDOT representatives.

### 4.2 Construction Approach

Our construction approach for the Floyd Hill project focuses on:

- Segmental bridge construction using balanced cantilever methods as specified in the design
- Controlled rock blasting operations with careful sequencing to minimize impact on traffic and environment
- Phased construction approach with East, Central, and West sections working in parallel
- Traffic management that maintains I-70 mobility during peak travel periods
- Implementation of all required environmental protections and wildlife crossing measures

We will mobilize specialized equipment and crews as needed and coordinate closely with subcontractors to ensure quality, safety, and schedule adherence.

### 4.3 Subcontractor Management

Kraemer's subcontractor management approach includes:
- Identifying and selecting subcontractors based on past performance, technical capability, and local availability
- Ensuring subcontractors meet the minimum 30% self-performance requirement
- Implementing a comprehensive DBE participation program that exceeds CDOT requirements
- Maintaining quality standards through regular oversight and performance metrics
