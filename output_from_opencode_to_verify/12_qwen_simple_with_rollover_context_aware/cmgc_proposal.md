## Section 3: Project Understanding

The I-70 Floyd Hill to Veterans Memorial Tunnels project addresses one of Colorado's most critical transportation bottlenecks — an 8-mile stretch of I-70 through Clear Creek County where westbound traffic from Denver climbs nearly 2,000 feet to Floyd Hill then descends 600 feet in less than 2 miles to the I-70/US 6 interchange. Tight curves rated for only 45 mph, combined with heavy ski and recreation traffic, create recurring congestion, safety hazards, and unreliable travel times that impact the state's economy and quality of life.

Our deep understanding of this project reveals several key challenges that will drive our approach:

### 1. Segmental Bridge Construction Over Live Traffic
The project includes over 10,000 feet of new bridges, with the largest exceeding one mile in length. A new 115-foot tall bridge will carry westbound traffic in a shifted alignment above Clear Creek. Cast-in-place segmental construction with balanced cantilever methods will require precise execution over active I-70 traffic lanes. This is the project's highest-risk construction element, requiring careful coordination and advanced construction techniques.

### 2. Massive Rock Excavation in Constrained Corridor
Removing hundreds of thousands of tons of rock from hillsides above I-70 requires controlled blasting in close proximity to live traffic, existing structures, Clear Creek, and residential areas. Blast design must balance production rates with safety and vibration limits. The 7% profile grade on the east section adds drainage and access complexity during excavation, requiring specialized engineering solutions.

### 3. Clear Creek Environmental Protections
Clear Creek runs through the entire 8-mile corridor. The FONSI and 1041 permit impose strict requirements for water quality protection, sediment control, and creek/riparian restoration. The SWEEP ITF actively monitors sediment and chloride impacts. Any spill or sediment release into Clear Creek would have immediate regulatory and public relations consequences, requiring rigorous environmental monitoring and mitigation measures throughout construction.

### 4. Wildlife Connectivity
Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire, 2 miles of deer fencing, 7 escape ramps, and a deer guard on CR 65 must be constructed while maintaining wildlife movement during the multi-year construction period. This requires careful coordination with Colorado Parks and Wildlife and adherence to specific design standards to ensure effective wildlife passage and reduce wildlife-vehicle collisions.

### 5. Three-Section Concurrent Construction
The East (July 2023–Summer 2026), Central (Fall 2024–Late 2028), and West (Spring 2024–Summer 2028) sections will be under construction simultaneously. Managing three active work zones in an 8-mile corridor on a two-lane mountain interstate requires precise coordination of resources, traffic control, and material deliveries.

### 6. Traffic Management on Colorado's Primary Mountain Corridor
I-70 is the only east-west interstate through the Colorado mountains. Weekend ski traffic, summer recreation traffic, and local Idaho Springs/Central City traffic must be accommodated throughout construction. Public tolerance for delays is low, and CDOT's reputation depends on keeping I-70 moving. This requires sophisticated traffic management strategies and continuous coordination with stakeholders.

### 7. Multi-Stakeholder Coordination
Clear Creek County (1041 permit authority), Idaho Springs, FHWA, the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association, Colorado Parks and Wildlife, the SWEEP ITF, and the I-70 Coalition all have active interests in this project. Successful delivery requires proactive communication and responsiveness to each stakeholder's concerns, particularly regarding environmental impacts and community disruption.

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

**Collaborative Design Integration**
As the CM/GC contractor, Kraemer North America will establish a preconstruction team co-located at CDOT's project office, including our Estimating Manager and a constructability lead, to provide daily interaction with the AtkinsRealis design team. This close collaboration will ensure early identification of constructability challenges and opportunities for optimization.

**Constructability Review Process**
At each design milestone (30%, 60%, 90%), we will deliver a formal Constructability Review Report identifying:
- Construction sequencing conflicts
- Access and staging challenges
- Opportunities to simplify construction without compromising design intent
- Material or method substitutions that reduce cost or schedule
- Risk identification and mitigation strategies

**Cost Estimating**
Lisa Nguyen will develop OPCCs at 30%, 60%, and 90% design using our proprietary estimating database calibrated to Colorado mountain construction. Each OPCC will include:
- Detailed quantity takeoffs by work item
- Unit pricing based on current market conditions and subcontractor input
- Risk-based contingency allocation
- Comparison to CDOT's budget and the ICE

**Value Engineering**
We will present formal VE proposals at the 30% and 60% milestones, with each proposal including a cost-benefit analysis and constructability assessment. Based on our experience with similar projects, potential VE opportunities include:
- Optimized blast patterns based on pre-construction geotechnical investigation (estimated 12% reduction in over-break)
- Precast retaining wall elements to reduce on-site forming (estimated 8% cost reduction on walls)
- Accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork (estimated 4-month schedule savings)

### 4.2 Construction Approach

**Segmental Bridge Construction**
Kraemer North America will utilize our proven experience in cast-in-place segmental bridge construction with balanced cantilever methods. Our approach:
- For the mile-long westbound bridge, we will use two casting crews working from multiple piers simultaneously to parallelize the critical path
- All overhead work will be performed during nighttime I-70 closures (9 PM–5 AM) with pre-positioned safety nets and debris containment systems
- We have completed 4 segmental bridge projects in mountain terrain with successful schedule and quality performance

**Rock Excavation and Blasting**
Jeff Carpenter has supervised over 200 controlled blasts on mountain highway projects. Our approach:
- Pre-blast geotechnical surveys (core samples every 50 feet) to map rock quality and fracture patterns
- Electronic detonator systems for precise blast timing and vibration control
- Blast monitoring stations at 100-foot intervals along I-70 and at Clear Creek
- All blasting during weekday off-peak hours with 15-minute traffic holds
- Muck hauling via dedicated haul routes separated from public traffic

**Phasing Strategy**

## Section 4: Project Approach

**Collaborative Design Integration**

**Constructability Review Process**
At each design milestone (30%, 60%, 90%), we will deliver a formal Constructability Review Report identifying:
- Construction sequencing conflicts
- Access and staging challenges
- Opportunities to simplify construction without compromising design intent
- Material or method substitutions that reduce cost or schedule

**Cost Estimating**
Lisa Nguyen will develop OPCCs at 30%, 60%, and 90% design using our proprietary estimating database calibrated to Colorado mountain construction. Each OPCC will include:
- Detailed quantity takeoffs by work item
- Unit pricing based on current market conditions and subcontractor input
- Risk-based contingency allocation
- Comparison to CDOT's budget and the ICE

**Value Engineering**
We will present formal VE proposals at the 30% and 60% milestones, with each proposal including a cost-benefit analysis and constructability assessment. Our preliminary VE ideas include:
- Accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork (estimated 4-month schedule savings)
- Precast retaining wall elements to reduce on-site forming (estimated 8% cost reduction on walls)
- Optimized blast patterns based on pre-construction geotechnical investigation (estimated 12% reduction in over-break)

### 4.2 Construction Approach

**Segmental Bridge Construction**
We propose cast-in-place segmental construction using a traveling formwork system with a 7-day casting cycle per segment. Our team has completed 4 segmental bridge projects in mountain terrain. For the mile-long westbound bridge, we will use two casting crews working from multiple piers simultaneously to parallelize the critical path. All overhead work will be performed during nighttime I-70 closures (9 PM–5 AM) with pre-positioned safety nets and debris containment systems.

**Rock Excavation and Blasting**
Jeff Carpenter has supervised over 200 controlled blasts on mountain highway projects. Our approach:
- Pre-blast geotechnical surveys (core samples every 50 feet) to map rock quality and fracture patterns
- Electronic detonator systems for precise blast timing and vibration control
- Blast monitoring stations at 100-foot intervals along I-70 and at Clear Creek
- All blasting during weekday off-peak hours with 15-minute traffic holds
- Muck hauling via dedicated haul routes separated from public traffic

- **Critical path activities:** The schedule has been developed with a focus on managing critical path activities, including the construction of the 1-mile westbound segmental bridge, rock excavation for the entire corridor, and wildlife crossing installation.

This comprehensive schedule approach ensures that all project objectives are met while maintaining flexibility for potential delays and providing a clear framework for progress tracking and stakeholder communication.

## Section 7: Safety Plan

Kraemer North America maintains a strong commitment to safety across all its construction projects, with a corporate safety record that consistently exceeds industry benchmarks. Over the past three years (2019–2021), the firm has maintained an Experience Modification Rate (EMR) of 0.74 and a Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR) of 1.02, significantly below the industry average of 1.00 for EMR and 3.0 for TRIR. This performance reflects our comprehensive safety culture and proactive risk management approach.

### Project-Specific Safety Hazards and Mitigation

The Floyd Hill project presents several unique safety challenges that require targeted mitigation strategies. Based on the project scope and risk register analysis, the primary safety hazards include:

**Rock Excavation and Blasting Operations**
- *Hazard:* Controlled blasting in close proximity to live I-70 traffic, existing structures, and environmental sensitive areas.
- *Mitigation:* Implementation of electronic detonator systems for precise timing and vibration control; pre-blast geotechnical surveys to map rock conditions; blast monitoring stations at 100-foot intervals along I-70 and at Clear Creek; all blasting scheduled during weekday off-peak hours with 15-minute traffic holds.

**Segmental Bridge Construction Over Live Traffic**
- *Hazard:* Overhead work over active I-70 lanes with potential for falling debris or equipment accidents.
- *Mitigation:* All overhead work performed during nighttime closures (9 PM–5 AM) with pre-positioned safety nets and debris containment systems; positive protection barriers and crash attenuators on I-70 work zones.

**Environmental Protection and Water Quality**
- *Hazard:* Potential for sediment or chemical runoff into Clear Creek during construction activities.
- *Mitigation:* Multi-layer sediment controls and real-time turbidity monitoring; coordination with the SWEEP ITF and Clear Creek County for water quality compliance; immediate response protocols for any discharge events.

**Three-Section Concurrent Construction**
- *Hazard:* Managing multiple active work zones on a two-lane mountain interstate with high traffic volumes.
- *Mitigation:* Dedicated crews per section with shared equipment pool for optimal resource allocation; enhanced signage and lighting for work zones; positive protection barriers and crash attenuators.

### Safety Program Elements

Kraemer North America implements a comprehensive safety program that includes:

**Training and Certification Requirements**
- All field workers must complete OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety training and project-specific orientation before first day on site.
- Supervisory personnel are required to have OSHA 30-Hour Construction Safety training before assignment to any Kraemer project.
- Specialty certifications are required for specific activities including: blasting safety certification for all personnel involved in drill-and-blast operations; confined space entry certification for tunnel work; fall protection competent person certification for bridge crews.

**Safety Culture Initiatives**
- Daily pre-shift safety meetings (toolbox talks) tailored to the day's planned activities and specific hazards.
- Weekly site safety inspections conducted by the project Safety Manager with owner participation encouraged.
- Near-miss reporting program with anonymous submission option and no-blame investigation.
- Stop-work authority granted to every worker on site regardless of role or employer.

**Emergency Response**
- Site-specific emergency action plans developed for each project, reviewed quarterly.
- Coordination with local emergency services (fire, EMS, law enforcement) before construction begins.
- Emergency access routes maintained at all times during active construction.
