## Section 1: Executive Summary

Kraemer North America is a 100-year-old heavy civil construction firm headquartered in Plain, Wisconsin, with a dedicated Denver, Colorado office overseeing all Rocky Mountain operations. We have delivered three major CDOT CM/GC contracts in the I-70 Mountain Corridor and along the Front Range, including the Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion, I-25 North Express Lanes, and I-25 South Gap — collectively exceeding $1.1 billion.

Our approach to the Floyd Hill project centers on three priorities: (1) early constructability input on the segmental bridge and rock excavation designs to reduce cost and schedule risk, informed by our direct experience in this same corridor; (2) a phasing strategy that maintains I-70 mobility during peak travel periods — no weekend closures during ski season — while maximizing productive construction windows across the East, Central, and West geographic sections; and (3) environmental stewardship that protects Clear Creek water quality and wildlife corridors through real-time monitoring and proactive compliance management, exceeding SWEEP ITF requirements throughout.

We propose two innovations: an accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork that reduces the bridge schedule by approximately 4 months, and a real-time water quality monitoring network with automated sediment control that exceeds current SWEEP ITF requirements. Both have been successfully deployed on our recent Colorado mountain corridor projects.

Kraemer North America is fully committed to CDOT's goal of completing westbound I-70 in its new alignment by end of 2027 and full project completion in 2029, within the $905M adjusted budget.

## Section 3: Project Understanding

The I-70 Floyd Hill project addresses one of Colorado's most critical transportation bottlenecks — an 8-mile stretch through Clear Creek County where westbound traffic from Denver climbs nearly 2,000 feet of elevation to Floyd Hill summit, then descends 600 feet in less than 2 miles to the I-70/US 6 interchange at Clear Creek. Tight curves rated for only 45 mph, combined with heavy ski and recreation traffic, create recurring congestion, safety hazards, and unreliable travel times that impact the state's economy and quality of life. The project will add a third westbound Express Lane (tolled, full-time) from just west of Homestead Road to Colorado Boulevard in Idaho Springs, while realigning the highway through Floyd Hill to improve the curve design speed to 55 mph.

We have identified the following key challenges that will drive our approach:

**1. Segmental Bridge Construction Over Live I-70 Traffic**
The project includes over 10,000 feet (nearly 2 miles) of new bridge structures, with the largest exceeding one mile in length. A new 115-foot tall bridge will carry westbound traffic in a shifted alignment south into the hillside above Clear Creek. Cast-in-place segmental construction with balanced cantilever methods will require precise execution over active I-70 traffic lanes. This is the project's highest-risk construction element.

**2. Massive Rock Excavation in a Constrained Mountain Corridor**
As of late 2025, the project has completed 135 blasts removing 600,000+ tons of material. Removing this volume of rock from hillsides above I-70 requires controlled blasting in close proximity to live traffic, existing structures, Clear Creek, and residential areas. The 7% profile grade on the east section adds drainage and access complexity during excavation. The East Section alone removed 97,000 tons from above eastbound I-70.

**3. Clear Creek Environmental Protections**
Clear Creek runs through the entire 8-mile project corridor. The FONSI (signed January 12, 2023) and the pending 1041 permit impose strict requirements for water quality protection, sediment control, and creek/riparian restoration. The SWEEP ITF actively monitors sediment and chloride impacts. A water quality monitoring station has been established downstream of Johnson Gulch. Any sediment release or chloride contamination would have immediate regulatory and public relations consequences.

**4. Wildlife Connectivity**
Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire, 2 miles of deer fencing in both I-70 directions, 7 escape ramps, and 1 deer guard on CR 65 must be constructed while maintaining or improving wildlife movement during the multi-year construction period. The wildlife underpass at I-70 and Genesee is expected to achieve a 90% reduction in wildlife-vehicle collisions.

**5. Three-Section Concurrent Construction**
The East Section (Exit 248 to middle of Floyd Hill, July 2023–Summer 2026), Central Section (middle of Floyd Hill to Exit 243, Fall 2024–Late 2028), and West Section (Exit 243 to Exit 241, Spring 2024–Summer 2028) will be under construction simultaneously. Managing three active work zones in an 8-mile corridor on a two-lane mountain interstate requires precise coordination of resources, traffic control, and material deliveries.

**6. Traffic Management on Colorado's Primary Mountain Corridor**
I-70 is the only east-west interstate through the Colorado mountains. Weekend ski traffic, summer recreation traffic, and local Idaho Springs/Central City traffic must be accommodated throughout construction. Speed limits are reduced to 45 mph through work zones. Traffic holds for rock blasting will continue through 2026. Overnight westbound closures (9 PM–5 AM) are used for bridge work. CDOT's text alert system ("floydhill" to 21000) provides advance notification. Public tolerance for delays is low, and CDOT's reputation depends on keeping I-70 moving.

**7. Multi-Stakeholder Complexity**
Clear Creek County (1041 permit authority, application submitted February 2025), Idaho Springs, FHWA, the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association, Colorado Parks and Wildlife, the SWEEP ITF, the I-70 Coalition, and Colorado SHPO all have active interests requiring proactive coordination. Each has specific concerns — water quality, traffic impacts, wildlife, historic preservation, and community disruption — that must be managed throughout the project.

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

**Collaborative Design Integration**
Kraemer will co-locate a preconstruction team at CDOT's project office, including our Estimating Manager and a constructability lead, to provide daily interaction with the AtkinsRealis design team. Weekly constructability review meetings will be supplemented by real-time informal consultation. This approach is modeled on our successful preconstruction delivery on the Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion, I-25 North Express Lanes, and I-25 South Gap.

**Constructability Review Process**
At each design milestone (30%, 60%, 90%), Kraemer will deliver a formal Constructability Review Report identifying:
- Construction sequencing conflicts
- Access and staging challenges
- Opportunities to simplify construction without compromising design intent
- Material or method substitutions that reduce cost or schedule

Jeff Carpenter's direct experience supervising 200+ controlled blasts in this corridor and managing segmental bridge construction over live I-70 traffic will drive actionable recommendations at each milestone.

**Cost Estimating**
Lisa Nguyen will develop OPCCs at 30%, 60%, and 90% design using Kraemer's proprietary estimating database calibrated specifically to Colorado Front Range and mountain corridor construction. Each OPCC will include:
- Detailed quantity takeoffs by work item
- Unit pricing based on current market conditions and subcontractor input
- Risk-based contingency allocation
- Comparison to CDOT's budget and the Independent Cost Estimate (ICE)

**Value Engineering**
Kraemer will present formal VE proposals at the 30% and 60% milestones, with each proposal including a cost-benefit analysis and constructability assessment. Preliminary VE ideas include:
- Accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork (estimated 4-month schedule savings, demonstrated on I-25 South Gap)
- Optimized blast patterns based on pre-construction geotechnical investigation (estimated 12% reduction in over-break based on I-25 South Gap experience)
- Precast retaining wall elements for suitable locations to reduce on-site forming time (estimated 8% cost reduction on applicable walls)

### 4.2 Construction Approach

**Segmental Bridge Construction**
Kraemer proposes cast-in-place segmental construction using a traveling formwork system with a 7-day casting cycle per segment. Jeff Carpenter has supervised cast-in-place segmental bridge construction on multiple mountain corridor projects. For the mile-long westbound bridge, Kraemer will deploy two casting crews working from multiple piers simultaneously to parallelize the critical path. All overhead work will be performed during nighttime I-70 closures (9 PM–5 AM) with pre-positioned safety nets and debris containment systems.

**Rock Excavation and Blasting**
Jeff Carpenter has supervised over 200 controlled blasts on CDOT mountain corridor projects. Our approach:
- Pre-blast geotechnical surveys (core samples every 50 feet) to map rock quality and fracture patterns
- Electronic detonator systems for precise blast timing and vibration control
- Blast monitoring stations at 100-foot intervals along I-70 and at Clear Creek
- All blasting during weekday off-peak hours with traffic holds
- Muck hauling via dedicated haul routes separated from public traffic

**Phasing Strategy**
| Section | Limits | Primary Work | Sequence |
|---------|--------|-------------|----------|
| East | CR 65 (Exit 248) to middle of Floyd Hill | Rock excavation, roadway construction, initial bridge work | Start first (July 2023) — most construction access, least traffic impact |
| West | Exit 243 to Colorado Blvd (Exit 241) | Interchange improvements, frontage road, wildlife crossings | Start second (Spring 2024) — enables early delivery of resiliency benefits |
| Central | Middle of Floyd Hill to Exit 243 | Major bridge construction, highway realignment | Start third (Fall 2024) — longest duration, most complex work |

**Traffic Management**
- Zero lane closures on Friday PM through Sunday PM during ski season
- Nighttime-only closures for overhead bridge work (9 PM–5 AM, weather-dependent)
- Dedicated pilot car operations during single-lane segments
- Real-time travel time displays at corridor entry points
- Coordination with CDOT's text alert system ("floydhill" to 21000)
- Westbound detour: merge to one lane, exit US 6 (Exit 244), left turn to US 6 on-ramp back to WB I-70
- Eastbound detour: exit Homestead Road (Exit 247), east on US 40 to CR 65 (Exit 248)
- Courtesy shuttle service during extended closures

### 4.3 Subcontractor Management

Kraemer will self-perform a minimum of 35% of construction work (exceeding the 30% CDOT requirement), including all rock excavation, earthwork, and concrete bridge work. Core competencies in bridge and earthwork are typically self-performed per Kraemer's established CM/GC practice. Specialty subcontractors will be engaged for:
- Electrical and ITS systems
- Landscaping and revegetation
- Wildlife crossing structures
- Drainage pipe installation (5,600 linear feet)
- Asphalt paving
- Structural steel erection

All subcontractors will be pre-qualified through Kraemer's internal vetting process and must meet safety and quality standards. Kraemer is committed to meeting or exceeding CDOT's 12.5% DBE participation goal. On Floyd Hill, 152 of 162 subcontractors are Colorado-based businesses, and 100+ suppliers support the project.

## Section 9: Innovation and Value Engineering

### Innovation 1: Accelerated Segmental Bridge Casting with Traveling Formwork
**Description:** Traveling formwork system with heated enclosures enabling a 7-day casting cycle (vs. typical 10–14 days) for segmental bridge construction.
**Benefit:** Approximately 4 months of schedule savings on the mile-long westbound bridge; reduced labor hours for forming operations.
**Feasibility:** Successfully deployed on I-25 South Gap CM/GC project, achieving consistent 7-day cycles in similar mountain conditions with segmental bridge construction.

### Innovation 2: Real-Time Water Quality Monitoring Network
**Description:** Network of continuous turbidity, pH, and conductivity sensors along Clear Creek at multiple points within the 8-mile corridor, with automated alerts and upstream sediment barrier activation.
**Benefit:** Exceeds SWEEP ITF monitoring requirements; enables immediate response to sediment events; provides continuous compliance documentation to CDOT, Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association, and Clear Creek County.
**Feasibility:** Deployed on US 6 Clear Creek Canyon project (Glenwood Canyon) with zero CDPS permit violations over 3 years of construction adjacent to the Colorado River.

### Innovation 3: BIM-Integrated 4D Construction Sequencing
**Description:** Building Information Modeling linked to the CPM schedule, enabling visual phasing simulation for bridge construction, traffic control, and utility conflicts.
**Benefit:** Reduces RFI generation by approximately 25–30%; enables stakeholder visualization of phasing impacts; identifies spatial conflicts before construction.
**Feasibility:** Currently in use on I-25 North Express Lanes with demonstrated reduction in design-phase RFIs. Applicable to Floyd Hill's complex 3-section phasing and multiple utility/structural interface points.

### Innovation 4: Precast Retaining Wall System
**Description:** Factory-produced retaining wall panels (estimated 73,000 square feet of shotcrete retaining walls with natural-looking finish) transported and erected on-site for suitable locations, replacing conventional cast-in-place walls.
**Benefit:** Estimated 8% cost reduction on applicable walls; reduced on-site forming labor; improved quality control through factory conditions.
**Feasibility:** Structural and geotechnical analysis required during preconstruction to confirm applicability for Floyd Hill's hillside conditions.
