## Section 3: Project Understanding

- The I‑70 Floyd Hill corridor presents steep grades, tight curves, heavy recreational traffic, and significant environmental constraints (water quality, wildlife, historic resources).
- Project scope includes addition of a third westbound lane, new bridge structures, realignment, and wildlife crossings.
- Environmental constraints are governed by NEPA (FONSI signed 12‑Jan‑2023), SWEEP ITF requirements, Section 106 consultation, and the 1041 permit.
- The project is funded primarily by federal INFRA grant ($100 M) and state SB 21‑260 ($5.4 B projected).

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

- Constructability reviews at 30 %, 60 %, and 90 % design milestones.
- Cost estimating using industry‑standard tools and independent cost estimates.
- Value engineering workshops with CDOT and design team.
- Regular progress reporting and communication plan.

### 4.2 Construction Approach

- Phased construction by geographic sections (East, Central, West) with detailed sequencing.
- Traffic management plan following CDOT traffic management guidelines; includes detours, lane closures, and real‑time traffic alerts.
- Use of cast‑in‑place segmental bridges with balanced cantilever construction and spliced concrete girders.
- Blasting and rock removal conducted with dust control and sediment barriers.
- Wildlife crossing construction per CDOT and Colorado Parks and Wildlife standards.

### 4.3 Subcontractor Management

- Subcontractor selection based on experience, past performance, and cost‑competitiveness.
- DBE participation plan to meet 49 CFR 26.
- Safety and quality monitoring integrated into subcontractor contracts.

## Section 5: Schedule

- Preconstruction schedule: constructability reviews at 30 % (July 2024), 60 % (Jan 2025), 90 % (Aug 2025).
- Construction schedule: East section July 2023 – Summer 2026, Central section Fall 2024 – Late 2028, West section Spring 2024 – Summer 2028.
- Critical‑path activities identified (e.g., bridge casting, blasting). 
- Schedule contingencies for weather, traffic, and environmental windows.

## Section 6: Risk Management

- Geotechnical risks: extensive rock scaling, blasting.
- Environmental risks: sediment and chloride runoff, wildlife impacts, historic resources.
- Traffic risks: high volume, ski traffic, limited work windows.
- Schedule risks: weather, equipment availability.
- Cost risks: material price volatility.
- Mitigation strategies and risk register in Appendix C.
