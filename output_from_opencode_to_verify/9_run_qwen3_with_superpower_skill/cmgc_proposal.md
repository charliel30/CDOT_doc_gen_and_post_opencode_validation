## Section 3: Project Understanding

### 3.1 Project Scope and Objectives

The Floyd Hill project represents one of the most significant highway improvements in Colorado's mountain corridor, addressing critical safety, capacity, and reliability issues on I-70 between Idaho Springs and Evergreen. The project's core objective is to transform the existing 2,000-foot elevation climb with dangerous curves into a 55 mph, 3-lane westbound corridor that provides reliable access through the mountain pass.

Key components of the scope include:
- Addition of a third westbound lane functioning as a full-time, tolled Express Lane from just west of Homestead Road (Exit 247) to Colorado Blvd./Idaho Springs (Exit 241)
- Highway realignment through Floyd Hill to straighten dangerous curves
- Over 10,000 feet of new bridges, including a nearly 1-mile long bridge carrying westbound traffic around Floyd Hill
- New 115-foot tall bridge carrying westbound traffic in a shifted alignment south into the hillside above Clear Creek
- Implementation of wildlife crossings and deer fencing to address the high rate of wildlife-vehicle collisions in the corridor
- 2-mile frontage road between US 6 and Hidden Valley/Central City Parkway (Exit 243)
- Clear Creek Greenway Trail improvements and resurfacing

### 3.2 Key Project Challenges

The Floyd Hill project presents several unique challenges that require specialized attention and strategic planning:

**Environmental Constraints:**
The project corridor runs through the Clear Creek watershed, requiring strict adherence to water quality protection measures. Clear Creek is a critical natural asset for the region, and the project must comply with SWEEP ITF requirements, maintain water quality monitoring programs, and implement sediment control measures throughout construction. The project must also comply with 1041 permit requirements from Clear Creek County, which govern projects affecting areas of state interest.

**Geotechnical and Construction Complexity:**
The project requires extensive rock excavation and blasting operations, with over 135 blasts planned and more than 600,000 tons of material to be removed. This includes 97,000 tons from the east section and 40,000+ tons from the central and west sections. The use of segmental bridge construction with balanced cantilever techniques requires specialized equipment and expertise. The steep grades and drainage requirements present significant engineering challenges that must be carefully managed.

**Traffic Management in Active Corridor:**
Construction must occur within an active interstate corridor with heavy recreational and commuter traffic. This requires careful phasing to minimize disruptions and maintain safety while ensuring mobility during peak travel periods. The project's traffic management plan must account for temporary speed limits, narrow lanes, shoulder closures, and detours, including a complex westbound detour that requires travelers to exit US 6 and re-enter I-70 via the US 6 on-ramp.

**Stakeholder Expectations and Community Impact:**
The project faces significant stakeholder scrutiny from local communities, environmental groups, and regional advocacy organizations. Key concerns include noise, dust, and vibration impacts on residents, traffic impacts on local roads, and the protection of wildlife corridors and historic resources. The project must also provide regular communication and coordination with multiple agencies including CDOT, FHWA, Clear Creek County, Colorado SHPO, and the I-70 Coalition.

### 3.3 Stakeholder Concerns and Requirements

The various stakeholders have specific requirements and expectations that must be addressed throughout the project lifecycle:

**CDOT's Expectations:**
CDOT's primary goal is to complete westbound I-70 in its new alignment by end of 2027 with full project completion by 2029, while delivering within the $905M adjusted budget. CDOT expects proactive constructability input, open-book cost transparency, and strong safety performance. The CM/GC contractor must demonstrate successful delivery of the CM/GC model to build confidence in this alternative delivery method.

**FHWA Requirements:**
FHWA requires full compliance with NEPA (FONSI conditions), federal-aid compliance including DBE and Buy America requirements, and adherence to 23 CFR Part 635 Subpart E for the CM/GC procurement process. The federal agency is particularly concerned about cost growth beyond the original estimate and timely obligation of the federal INFRA grant funds ($100M).

**Environmental and Community Concerns:**
Environmental groups and local communities are particularly concerned about water quality impacts on Clear Creek, noise and dust impacts on residents, and the long-term maintenance of improvements. The Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association and SWEEP ITF require continuous water quality monitoring, immediate notification of discharge events, and restoration of disturbed riparian areas. Local residents expect advance notification of blasting schedules and vibration monitoring near residences.

**Historic Preservation Requirements:**
Section 106 compliance with the Colorado State Historic Preservation Office is required, particularly for the identified archaeological site. A treatment plan and memorandum of agreement with SHPO are necessary before work can proceed in affected areas.

### 3.4 Project Context and Significance

The Floyd Hill project is of paramount importance to Colorado's transportation system and regional economy. As one of the worst recurring bottlenecks on I-70, it serves critical travel needs for commuters, tourists, and recreational users. The project's completion will provide a more reliable, safer, and higher-capacity corridor through the mountain pass that connects Denver to the western Colorado region.

This project represents the first major CM/GC delivery in Colorado and sets a precedent for how state highway projects can be delivered more efficiently and collaboratively. The success of the Floyd Hill project will directly impact the state's ability to deliver future mountain corridor improvements and could influence broader adoption of CM/GC delivery methods across CDOT's portfolio.

## Section 4: Project Approach

### 4.1 Preconstruction Approach

Our approach to the Floyd Hill project begins with extensive collaboration with CDOT's design team and a commitment to proactive constructability input. We have already established our preconstruction approach through our involvement in three previous CDOT CM/GC projects, including the I-25 South Gap and I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion, where we provided valuable input on design optimization and cost reduction.

Our preconstruction process will focus on three key areas:

**Constructability Review Process:**
We will conduct detailed constructability reviews at each design milestone (30%, 60%, 90%) to identify potential issues early and optimize both design and construction methods. This includes our standard approach of providing design alternatives to reduce construction costs or schedule risks, particularly around the segmental bridge design where we have extensive experience in the I-70 corridor.

**Cost Estimating Methodology:**
Our estimating approach will be fully open-book with CDOT, leveraging our proven methodology from the I-25 South Gap and I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels projects. Our estimating manager, Lisa Nguyen, has led OPCC development and CAP negotiation support on these projects, and she has developed a comprehensive estimating database calibrated specifically to Colorado Front Range mountain construction conditions. She will apply this same approach to Floyd Hill, ensuring that all cost components are transparent and well-documented.

**Value Engineering:**
We will actively pursue value engineering opportunities that can deliver cost savings or schedule acceleration without compromising quality or safety. Our experience with segmental bridge construction and rock excavation in mountain environments has yielded several proven techniques, including:
- Accelerated segmental bridge casting cycle using traveling formwork that reduces bridge schedule by approximately 4 months
- Real-time water quality monitoring system with automated sediment control that exceeds SWEEP ITF requirements

**Communication and Reporting:**
We will implement a comprehensive communication plan that includes weekly status reports, monthly stakeholder meetings, and real-time updates on any issues that may impact the project schedule or budget. All communications will be documented and shared with CDOT in a timely manner.

### 4.2 Construction Approach

Our construction approach for Floyd Hill is designed to address the unique challenges of the project while maintaining the highest standards of safety, quality, and environmental protection.

**Means and Methods for Major Work Elements:**
For the extensive rock excavation required on this project, we will implement a comprehensive blasting program that leverages our experience from the I-25 South Gap project. Our superintendent, Jeff Carpenter, has personally supervised over 200 controlled blasts on CDOT projects. This includes the use of advanced blasting techniques such as pre-splitting and electronic detonators for precise timing, with careful attention to minimizing vibration impacts on nearby residential areas and sensitive environmental zones.

For segmental bridge construction, we will employ the balanced cantilever method with traveling formwork, which allows for faster construction and reduced exposure of workers to active traffic. Our experience with this approach on the I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels Expansion project will be directly leveraged to optimize this critical construction element.

**Phasing Strategy and Sequencing:**
We will implement a strategic phasing approach that allows for the continuation of I-70 operations throughout construction. Based on the project schedule, we will execute construction in three geographic sections (East, Central, West) with careful sequencing to minimize traffic impacts. Our phasing strategy will:
- Prioritize work in the central section during peak ski season to avoid impact on recreational traffic
- Schedule the most disruptive activities such as major blasting and overnight closures during off-peak hours
- Maintain continuous access for emergency services and local traffic
- Implement staged opening of new lanes to maximize mobility during construction

**Traffic Management:**
Our traffic management approach will be designed to minimize disruptions to travelers while maintaining safety. We will leverage our experience with the I-70 Veterans Memorial Tunnels project to implement:
- Real-time traffic monitoring and adjustments to construction timing
- Extended coordination with local law enforcement during peak periods
- Maintenance of emergency access lanes at all times
- A comprehensive communication system that includes text alerts and digital signage

**Working in Active I-70 Corridor:**
Working in a live interstate corridor requires extensive planning and execution expertise. Our approach will include:
- Coordinating closely with CDOT's traffic management team to ensure maximum mobility
- Implementing comprehensive safety barriers and signage
- Scheduling critical construction activities during periods of least traffic impact
- Maintaining dedicated safety personnel at all times in active work zones

**Equipment and Resource Mobilization:**
We will mobilize specialized equipment for the unique challenges of this project, including:
- Heavy-duty blasting equipment and rock handling systems for the extensive excavation
- Segmental bridge formwork systems and specialized lifting equipment
- Advanced environmental monitoring systems for water quality and air quality

## Section 5: Schedule

### 5.1 Preconstruction Schedule

Our preconstruction schedule is designed to provide maximum value to CDOT through early constructability input and comprehensive planning that reduces risk and improves delivery outcomes. Based on the project timeline and CDOT's requirements, we propose a preconstruction phase that will last approximately 18 months, beginning with the project kickoff in July 2023.

**Key Preconstruction Milestones:**
- **Months 1-3 (July-September 2023):** Initial project kickoff, team mobilization, and site reconnaissance. We will conduct detailed geotechnical and environmental assessments and begin constructability reviews of the design at the 30% milestone.
- **Months 4-6 (October-December 2023):** Continued constructability reviews and design optimization at the 60% milestone. We will finalize our estimating approach and begin cost modeling for the major construction elements.
- **Months 7-9 (January-March 2024):** Advanced constructability reviews and design optimization at the 90% milestone. We will complete our cost estimating and begin finalizing our CAP negotiation strategy.
- **Months 10-12 (April-June 2024):** Finalizing the CAP and completing all preconstruction deliverables. We will begin preparations for the construction phase and establish our field office and staging areas.
- **Months 13-18 (July 2024-June 2025):** Ongoing preconstruction support for construction start-up and early project planning.

**Constructability Reviews:**
Our approach will include detailed constructability reviews at each major design milestone:
- 30% Design Milestone: Focus on rock excavation methods, bridge approach design, and environmental mitigation strategies
- 60% Design Milestone: Focus on detailed bridge construction methods, retaining wall design, and traffic management plans
- 90% Design Milestone: Focus on final construction sequencing, equipment mobilization plans, and project closeout strategies

**Cost Estimating Deliverables:**
- Monthly cost estimate deliverables aligned with design completion (30%, 60%, 90%)
- Detailed quantity takeoffs for rock excavation, bridge construction, and environmental mitigation
- Subcontractor cost modeling and market analysis
- Risk-adjusted cost estimates for the major project elements

### 5.2 Construction Schedule

Our proposed construction schedule is structured around the three geographic sections identified for the project and is designed to minimize traffic impacts while meeting CDOT's key milestones.

**Geographic Section Timeline:**
- **East Section (CR 65 to middle of Floyd Hill):** Construction began in July 2023, with expected completion by summer 2026. This section includes the initial rock excavation, bridge construction, and early pavement work.
- **Central Section (Middle of Floyd Hill to Exit 243):** Construction began in Fall 2024, with expected completion by late 2028. This section includes the more complex segmental bridge construction over live traffic and significant rock excavation work.
- **West Section (Exit 243 to Colorado Blvd):** Construction began in Spring 2024, with expected completion by summer 2028. This section includes the final bridge construction and pavement work.

**Major Construction Milestones:**
- **End of 2025:** Approximately 3 miles of new pavement between CR 65 and middle of Floyd Hill substantially complete
- **End of 2026:** Segmental bridge construction over live traffic begins and continues through 2027
- **End of 2027:** Westbound I-70 in new alignment
- **End of 2028:** Eastbound I-70 in new alignment
- **End of 2029:** Full project completion

**Seasonal Considerations:**
We will carefully account for seasonal constraints in our schedule:
- Winter weather: 15 working days weather float per year, with no critical-path work scheduled December–February
- Peak ski season: Schedule major construction activities to avoid impact on recreational traffic
- Summer construction: Take advantage of extended daylight hours for work activities

### 5.3 Schedule Risk and Contingency

We have identified and quantified several key schedule risks and have developed appropriate mitigation strategies and contingency plans.

**Key Schedule Risks:**
Based on our risk assessment, the top schedule risks include:
1. **Winter weather delays exceeding planned float** (Risk S-1): With 15 working days of weather float per year and no critical-path work during winter months, we have adequate protection against weather delays.
2. **Segmental bridge casting productivity lower than planned** (Risk S-2): We have planned for this with backup casting crews, heated enclosures for cold weather, and a mock-up casting prior to production.
3. **Supply chain disruption for structural materials** (Risk S-3): Early procurement of long-lead items, multiple supplier sourcing, and material storage staging areas provide mitigation.
4. **Concurrent 3-section construction creates resource conflicts** (Risk S-4): Dedicated crews per section with a shared equipment pool and utilization tracking will minimize conflicts.
5. **Environmental windows restrict construction activities** (Risk S-5): The schedule incorporates seasonal restrictions and prioritizes non-restricted work during restricted periods.

**Schedule Recovery Approach:**
In the event of schedule delays, we will implement the following recovery strategies:
- Identify and fast-track critical path activities
- Implement additional crews or equipment as needed
- Adjust non-critical activities to maintain overall project momentum
- Maintain close communication with CDOT on any schedule impacts

**Contingency Planning:**
We have included appropriate contingency in our schedule:
- 15 working days weather float per year
- Contingency for bridge construction productivity
- Resource allocation flexibility to address conflicts between sections
