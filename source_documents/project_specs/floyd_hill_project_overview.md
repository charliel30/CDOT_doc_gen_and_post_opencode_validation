# I-70 Floyd Hill to Veterans Memorial Tunnels — Project Overview

## Project Identification

- **Project Name:** I-70 Floyd Hill to Veterans Memorial Tunnels Improvements
- **Location:** I-70 Mountain Corridor, Mile Points 241–248, Clear Creek County, Colorado
- **Limits:** County Road 65 (Exit 248, west of Evergreen) to Colorado Boulevard (Exit 241, eastern Idaho Springs)
- **Length:** Approximately 8 miles
- **Project Delivery:** Construction Manager/General Contractor (CM/GC)
- **CM/GC Contractor:** Kraemer North America
- **Lead Designer / Engineer of Record:** AtkinsRealis (formerly Atkins)
- **Environmental/Planning Consultant:** HDR Inc.
- **NEPA Status:** Finding of No Significant Impact (FONSI) signed January 12, 2023

---

## Project Need

Westbound vehicles climbing from Denver gain nearly 2,000 feet of elevation to the top of Floyd Hill, then descend 600 feet in less than 2 miles to the I-70/US 6 interchange at Clear Creek. This steep grade, combined with tight curves (existing bridges rated for only 45 mph) and heavy ski/recreation traffic, creates one of the worst recurring bottlenecks on I-70. The project addresses safety, capacity, and reliability for this critical mountain corridor.

---

## Scope of Work

### Roadway and Lanes
- Addition of a third westbound lane functioning as a full-time, tolled Express Lane from just west of Homestead Road (Exit 247) to Colorado Blvd./Idaho Springs (Exit 241)
- Highway realignment through Floyd Hill to straighten dangerous curves
- New curve design speed: 55 mph (up from 45 mph)

### Bridge Structures
- Over 10,000 feet (nearly 2 miles) of new bridges
- Largest single bridge: over 1 mile long, carrying westbound traffic around Floyd Hill
- New 115-foot tall bridge carrying westbound traffic in a shifted alignment south into the hillside above Clear Creek
- Construction types:
  - Cast-in-place segmental bridges with balanced cantilever construction
  - Spliced concrete girders
- Nearly 4 miles of retaining walls
- 73,000 square feet of shotcrete on retaining walls (natural-looking finish)

### Interchanges and Connectivity
- Two-mile frontage road between US 6 and Hidden Valley/Central City Parkway (Exit 243)
- Roundabout at County Road 65 (Exit 248) on US 40
- Roundabout at Homestead Road (Exit 247) on US 40
- Roundabout at I-70/Hidden Valley/Central City Parkway (Exit 243) interchange
- Relocated left-merge US 6 on-ramp to Exit 243
- Extended eastbound I-70 on-ramp from US 6

### Recreation and Trail
- Improved and resurfaced Clear Creek Greenway Trail with concrete surface
- Trail alignment on south side of Clear Creek; frontage road on north side

### Wildlife Crossings
- Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire locations
- 2 miles of deer fencing in both directions between Soda Creek and Homestead roads
- 7 escape ramps
- 1 deer guard on County Road 65
- Wildlife underpass at I-70 and Genesee (expected 90% reduction in wildlife-vehicle collisions)

### Other Infrastructure
- 2 permanent air quality monitors (Floyd Hill and Idaho Springs)
- Rural broadband coordination (access to conduits and fiber in interstate right-of-way)
- 5,600 linear feet of drainage pipe
- Water quality monitoring station downstream of Johnson Gulch

## Geographic and Engineering Details

### Terrain
- Westbound climb of ~2,000 feet elevation to Floyd Hill summit
- 600-foot descent in less than 2 miles from Floyd Hill to Clear Creek
- 7% profile grade on east section (significant drainage engineering challenge)
- Clear Creek runs through the entire project corridor

### Geotechnical Conditions
- Extensive rock scaling and blasting required throughout corridor
- East Section: 97,000 tons of material removed from hillside above eastbound I-70
- Central/West Sections: 40,000+ tons removed via 17 blasts (as of late 2024)
- Total (late 2025): 135 blasts, 600,000+ tons of material removed
- Shotcrete used on retaining walls for structural reinforcement and aesthetics

## Traffic Management During Construction

- Speed limits reduced to 45 mph through work zones
- Narrower lanes and shoulders during construction
- Lane restriping, shoulder closures, temporary barriers
- I-70 traffic holds for rock blasting through 2026 (weekday off-peak hours)
- Overnight westbound closures for bridge work (9 PM–5 AM, weather-dependent)
- Westbound detour: merge to one lane, exit US 6 (Exit 244), left turn to US 6 on-ramp back to WB I-70
- Eastbound detour: exit Homestead Road (Exit 247), east on US 40 to CR 65 (Exit 248)
- Text alert system: text "floydhill" to 21000
- Courtesy shuttle service (650 trips April–September 2024)

## Design Criteria and Standards

- CM/GC delivery to optimize cost and constructability
- Cast-in-place segmental bridge with balanced cantilever construction
- Spliced concrete girders where appropriate
- Curve design speed: 55 mph (improved from 45 mph)
- 7% profile grade accommodated for drainage
- Shotcrete retaining walls with natural-looking finish
- Concrete surface for Clear Creek Greenway Trail
- Wildlife crossing design per Colorado Parks and Wildlife / CDOT standards
