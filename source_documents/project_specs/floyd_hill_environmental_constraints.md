# I-70 Floyd Hill — Environmental Constraints and Mitigation Requirements

## NEPA Process

- **Environmental Assessment (EA):** Released August 2021
- **Finding of No Significant Impact (FONSI):** Signed January 12, 2023 by FHWA
- FONSI based on EA, environmental reevaluation of the CM/GC Refined Preferred Alternative, and proposed mitigation measures in Appendix B
- Alternatives evaluated and dismissed:
  - Canyon viaduct structure (elevating I-70 through Clear Creek Canyon)
  - Tunnel alternative
  - Selected: highway realignment with new bridge structures

---

## Water Quality

### Constraints
- Clear Creek runs through the entire 8-mile project corridor
- Winter highway maintenance requires salt and liquid deicers — chloride runoff impacts Clear Creek
- Stormwater runoff carrying construction sediment threatens aquatic habitats
- Managing sediment and chloride runoff is a top priority for the SWEEP ITF (Sediment/Water Quality/Environment/Erosion Protection Interagency Task Force)

### Mitigation Requirements
- Water quality monitoring program developed by CDOT and approved by Clear Creek County
- Fifth continuous water quality monitoring station added downstream of Johnson Gulch
- Annual reporting of monitoring data to CSS (Context Sensitive Solutions) participants and the Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association (UCCWA)
- Sediment control measures throughout construction
- Creek and riparian area restoration near wildlife crossings

---

## Air Quality

- Two permanent air quality monitors installed at Floyd Hill and Idaho Springs
- Monitors collect data on local conditions and long-term trends
- Dust control measures required during rock blasting and earthwork

---

## Wildlife

### Constraints
- High rate of wildlife-vehicle collisions in corridor
- Wildlife movement corridors cross I-70 at multiple points

### Mitigation
- Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire locations
- Wildlife underpass at I-70 and Genesee (expected 90% reduction in wildlife-vehicle collisions)
- 2 miles of deer fencing in both I-70 directions between Soda Creek and Homestead roads
- 7 escape ramps to allow trapped animals to exit the highway
- 1 deer guard on County Road 65
- Design per Colorado Parks and Wildlife / CDOT standards

---

## Historic Preservation (Section 106)

- Section 106 consultation conducted with Colorado State Historic Preservation Office (SHPO)
- New construction impacts to an archaeological site identified
- Treatment plan required
- Memorandum of agreement with SHPO required before work can proceed in affected areas

---

## Clear Creek County 1041 Permit

- Required under Colorado's Areas and Activities of State Interest (1041) regulations
- Application submitted February 2025
- Requires description of:
  - How mitigation measures will be implemented
  - When mitigation measures will be implemented
  - How mitigation measures will be financed
- County has authority over projects affecting areas of state interest within their jurisdiction
