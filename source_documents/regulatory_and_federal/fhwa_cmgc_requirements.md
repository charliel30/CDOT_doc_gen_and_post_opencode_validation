# Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) — CM/GC Requirements

## Legal Authority

### MAP-21 (Moving Ahead for Progress in the 21st Century Act, 2012)
- Section 1303 formally authorized CM/GC as an approved project delivery method for federal-aid highway projects
- Eliminated the need for Special Experimental Project (SEP-14) approval for CM/GC use
- Requires compliance with all other federal-aid requirements (DBE, Buy America, prevailing wage, etc.)

### Final Rule (December 2, 2016)
- Implements the MAP-21 statutory provisions governing CM/GC contracting
- Published in 23 CFR Part 635 Subpart E
- Establishes FHWA approval requirements for CM/GC projects receiving federal funding

---

## Key Federal Requirements for CM/GC Projects

### FHWA Approval
- State DOTs must obtain FHWA approval before using CM/GC delivery on federally funded projects
- Approval process requires the state to demonstrate the project is appropriate for CM/GC delivery
- FHWA reviews the procurement process, contract terms, and price negotiation procedures

### Procurement
- CM/GC must be selected based on qualifications, experience, past performance, or best-value criteria
- Selection cannot be based solely on lowest price
- The procurement process must be competitive and transparent
- All evaluation criteria must be published in the solicitation documents

### Price Negotiation
- The construction price (GMP/CAP) must be negotiated, not competitively bid
- An Independent Cost Estimate (ICE) is required to validate the negotiated price
- If the state and CM cannot agree on a price, the state must have the option to terminate and competitively bid the construction work
- All negotiations must follow open-book pricing principles — the owner has access to all cost data

### Federal-Aid Compliance
- **DBE (Disadvantaged Business Enterprise):** CM/GC projects must meet the same DBE requirements as traditional projects (49 CFR Part 26)
- **Buy America:** Steel, iron, and manufactured products must be produced in the United States (23 CFR 635.410)
- **Davis-Bacon Act:** All laborers and mechanics must be paid prevailing wage rates
- **NEPA:** Environmental review must be completed before construction authorization, though preconstruction services can begin before NEPA completion
- **Uniform Audit Requirements:** Federal funds are subject to audit per 2 CFR Part 200

## CM/GC Process as Defined by FHWA

### Phase 1: Preconstruction Services
- CM provides constructability input during design
- CM develops cost estimates at various design milestones (30%, 60%, 90%)
- CM identifies risks, scheduling constraints, and value engineering opportunities
- NEPA process can proceed in parallel
- Preconstruction services are contracted separately from construction

### Phase 2: Price Negotiation
- At approximately 60-90% design completion, the CM submits a GMP/CAP proposal
- Owner compares against the Independent Cost Estimate
- Open-book negotiations: owner has full visibility into all cost components
- If negotiations fail, the owner may terminate and rebid the construction work competitively

### Phase 3: Construction
- Upon successful price negotiation, the CM transitions to General Contractor
- Construction proceeds under a GMP/CAP contract
- Standard federal-aid construction oversight applies
- Change order procedures follow federal guidelines

## Distinction from Other Delivery Methods

| Feature | Design-Bid-Build | CM/GC | Design-Build |
|---------|-----------------|-------|-------------|
| Contractor input during design | No | Yes | Yes |
| Separate design and construction contracts | Yes | Yes (initially) | No |
| Price determination | Competitive low bid | Negotiated GMP/CAP | Competitive (price + technical) |
| Owner design control | Full | Full | Limited |
| Construction start | After 100% design | After price negotiation (~60-90% design) | Can begin before 100% design |
| Risk allocation | Mostly owner | Shared | Mostly contractor |

## CDOT-Specific Application of Federal Rules

- CDOT's CM/GC Manual (Volume 25) implements these federal requirements at the state level
- CDOT uses a two-step best-value selection process (RFQ → short list → RFP)
- CDOT terms the negotiated price as "GC CAP" (General Contractor Construction Agreed-upon Price)
- The Colorado Transportation Commission must approve the use of CM/GC delivery for each project
- CDOT's 8 evaluation factors for CM/GC selection align with FHWA's best-value requirements:
  1. Delivery Schedule
  2. Project Complexity and Innovation
  3. Level of Design
  4. Initial Project Risk
  5. Cost
  6. Staff Experience/Availability
  7. Level of Oversight and Control
  8. Competition and Contractor Experience
