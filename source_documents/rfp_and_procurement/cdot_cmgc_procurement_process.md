# CDOT CM/GC Procurement Process

This document describes how CDOT procures CM/GC contractors, based on CDOT's CM/GC Manual, published RFPs, and federal guidelines.

---

## Overview

CDOT uses a **two-step best-value selection** process for CM/GC projects:
1. **Step 1 (RFQ):** Request for Qualifications → short list of ~3 firms
2. **Step 2 (RFP):** Request for Proposals → evaluation, interviews, and award

The entire process from RFP preparation to contract execution typically takes 4–6 months.

---

## Step 1: Request for Qualifications (RFQ)

### Purpose
Narrow the field of interested contractors to a short list of the most qualified firms.

### What Contractors Submit (Statement of Qualifications / SOQ)
- Firm overview and organizational structure
- Relevant project experience (similar size, scope, and complexity)
- Key personnel qualifications and availability
- Financial capacity and bonding capability
- Safety record (Experience Modification Rate, OSHA rates)
- DBE participation history and plan
- References from prior owners

### Evaluation
- Reviewed by a CDOT Selection Panel
- Scored against published criteria (qualifications-based, no price at this stage)
- Typically 3 firms are short-listed and invited to submit full proposals

---

## Step 2: Request for Proposals (RFP)

### RFP Timeline
| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| RFP preparation by CDOT | 2–8 weeks |
| Proposal preparation by contractors | 4–6 weeks |
| CDOT evaluation and interviews | 3–6 weeks |
| Final selection and contract execution | 4–6 weeks |

### What the RFP Contains
- Project description and scope of work
- Proposal content requirements (Section 3 of typical CDOT RFPs)
- Evaluation criteria and relative weights
- Contract terms and conditions
- Preconstruction services scope
- Self-performance requirements
- DBE goals
- Insurance and bonding requirements

### Proposal Content (Two Envelopes)

#### Envelope 1: Technical Proposal
Evaluated first. Typically divided into identifiable and non-identifiable sections:

**Identifiable Section (firm can be identified):**
- Key Personnel — resumes, qualifications, roles, availability
- Organizational Chart — project team structure, reporting lines
- Relevant Experience — past projects of similar scope and complexity
- References

**Non-Identifiable Section (firm name redacted during evaluation):**
- Project Understanding — demonstration of knowledge of project challenges and context
- Project Approach — construction methodology, means and methods, phasing strategy
- Constructability Input — ideas for improving the design based on construction expertise
- Schedule — preliminary construction schedule with milestones and phasing
- Risk Management — identification of key risks and mitigation strategies
- Safety Plan — approach to worker and public safety during construction
- Quality Management — quality assurance/quality control procedures
- Innovation — value engineering ideas, novel approaches, or technology

**Format Requirements (typical):**
- Page limit: 30–50 pages (varies by project)
- Font: 12-point Times New Roman or Arial (10-point minimum for charts/tables)
- Single-spaced
- Sequentially numbered pages

#### Envelope 2: Price Proposal
Opened only after technical evaluation is complete.

- **CM Preconstruction Services Fee:** The cost for advisory services during design
- **CM/GC Management Fee Percentage:** The contractor's markup on construction costs
- Fee schedules for key personnel during preconstruction
- Does NOT include the full construction price (that is negotiated later as the CAP)

### Evaluation Process

1. **Technical Evaluation:** Selection Panel scores technical proposals against published criteria
2. **Interviews:** Short-listed firms present to the Selection Panel and answer questions
3. **Price Evaluation:** Price proposals are opened and scored
4. **Best Value Calculation:** Technical score and price score are combined per the formula in the RFP
5. **Recommendation:** Panel recommends the top-ranked proposer to the Chief Engineer
6. **Award:** Chief Engineer approves; contract is executed

### Evaluation Ratings (Technical)

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Exceptional / Outstanding | Significantly exceeds requirements; innovative approach, very low risk |
| Acceptable / Satisfactory | Meets requirements; adequate approach, manageable risk |
| Marginal / Deficient | Below requirements but correctable; notable gaps or risks |
| Unacceptable | Fails to meet requirements; cannot be corrected |

---

## Post-Award: Preconstruction Phase

After the CM is selected and the preconstruction contract is executed:

1. **NTP Issued:** Notice to Proceed starts the preconstruction clock
2. **Collaborative Design:** CM works with CDOT's design team to provide constructability input
3. **Cost Estimating:** CM provides OPCCs (Opinions of Probable Construction Cost) at design milestones (30%, 60%, 90%)
4. **Risk Identification:** CM identifies construction risks and proposes mitigation
5. **Value Engineering:** CM suggests design modifications to reduce cost or improve schedule
6. **ICE Development:** Independent Cost Estimator provides benchmark pricing

---

## Post-Award: CAP Negotiation

When design reaches ~60–90% completion:

1. **CM Submits GC CAP Proposal:** Detailed cost breakdown using open-book pricing
2. **CDOT Reviews:** Compares against ICE and OPCC history
3. **Negotiation:** Open-book review of all cost components (labor, materials, equipment, subs, overhead)
4. **Agreement:** If both parties agree, CDOT executes a GC Construction Contract at the negotiated CAP
5. **Failure to Agree:** If negotiations fail, CDOT can terminate and competitively bid the construction

---

## Self-Performance Requirements

CDOT enforces minimum self-performance thresholds:

| Phase | Minimum Self-Performance |
|-------|------------------------|
| Preconstruction CM Services | 75% of preconstruction work |
| Construction (GC Phase) | 30% of total construction work |

Self-performed work must be done by the contractor's own staff, not through subcontractors. Specialized services (e.g., utility relocation, environmental remediation) may be excluded from the calculation.

---

## Prequalification

- General contractors must be prequalified in CDOT's B2GNow system
- Application must be submitted at least 17 calendar days before bid due date
- Subcontractors must register in B2GNow but do not need prequalification
- Professional consulting firms providing design services must also prequalify through B2GNow
