## 1. Project Description

### 1.1 Overview
The Colorado Department of Transportation (CDOT) is seeking proposals from qualified contractors to provide Construction Manager (CM) services during the preconstruction phase and, upon successful negotiation of a Construction Agreed-upon Price (CAP), to serve as General Contractor (GC) for the I-70 Floyd Hill to Veterans Memorial Tunnels Improvements Project.

### 1.2 Project Location
Interstate 70 Mountain Corridor, Mile Points 241 to 248, Clear Creek County, Colorado. From County Road 65 (Exit 248) west of Evergreen to Colorado Boulevard (Exit 241) at eastern Idaho Springs. Approximately 8 miles.

### 1.3 Project Scope
The project includes:
- Addition of a westbound Express Lane (tolled, full-time)
- Highway realignment through Floyd Hill
- Over 10,000 feet of new bridge structures including a 1+ mile westbound viaduct
- Nearly 4 miles of retaining walls
- Two-mile frontage road between US 6 and Exit 243
- Roundabouts at Exits 248, 247, and 243
- Clear Creek Greenway Trail improvements
- Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire
- Air quality monitoring infrastructure

### 1.4 Estimated Project Cost
$700,000,000 (2020 estimate, subject to adjustment)

### 1.5 Project Delivery Method
Construction Manager/General Contractor (CM/GC) as approved by the Colorado Transportation Commission, June 2021.

### 1.6 NEPA Status
Environmental Assessment released August 2021. Finding of No Significant Impact (FONSI) anticipated Q1 2023.

## 3. Proposal Content Requirements

### 3.1 General Requirements
- Technical Proposal and Price Proposal shall be submitted in separate sealed envelopes
- Font: No smaller than 11-point font
- Single-spaced, 1-inch margins, 8.5" x 11" paper (one page may be 11" x 17")
- Typewritten responses required
- Sequential page numbering
- Electronic submission in PDF format

**Page Limits:**
- Part 1 (Identifiable Submittal): No more than 6 one-sided pages
- Part 2 (Non-Identifiable Submittal): No more than 6 single-sided pages
- CMGC Management Price Percentage Proposal: Submitted only by short-listed firms at interview stage

*Note: For large, complex projects like Floyd Hill, page limits may be expanded. Some CDOT CM/GC RFPs allow 30-50 pages total. The page limits above reflect CDOT's standard format; the actual Floyd Hill RFP may have allowed more.*

### 3.2 Technical Proposal Content

The technical proposal is divided into identifiable and non-identifiable submittals. Non-identifiable sections are evaluated blind (firm name redacted) to reduce bias.

**Part 1: Identifiable Submittal**

*Section 3.1 — Composition and Commitment of Key Personnel (scored separately)*
- Description of the composition of Project Key Personnel
- If Joint Venture or association, indicate specific responsibilities of each team member
- Organizational chart showing project team structure and CDOT interface
- Resumes for all key personnel (1 page per person maximum):
  - Project Manager / Construction Manager
  - Project Superintendent
  - Estimating Manager
  - Scheduling Manager
  - Safety Manager
  - Quality Manager
  - Environmental Compliance Manager
- Statement of personnel availability and commitment
- Identification of any personnel shared with other projects

**Part 2: Non-Identifiable Submittal**

*Section 3.2 — The following categories are evaluated:*

- **Approach to Risk** — Identification of project-specific risks and mitigation strategies; risk tracking and communication protocols; how risks will be addressed during preconstruction to reduce cost and schedule exposure
- **Schedule** — Preconstruction and construction schedules; critical path identification; seasonal constraints; phasing strategy; milestone alignment with CDOT targets
- **Strategic Project Approach** — Preconstruction services approach (collaboration, constructability, estimating, VE); construction means and methods; traffic management; subcontractor management; self-performance plan (minimum 30% construction, 75% preconstruction); environmental compliance approach
- **Project Innovations** — Value engineering ideas; innovative technologies or methods; past examples of successful innovation; quantified benefits

*Blanket proprietary designations are permitted for Approach to Risk, Schedule, Strategic Project Approach, Project Innovations, and CMGC Management Price sections.*

**Part 3: CMGC Management Price Percentage Proposal**

*Section 3.3 — Submitted only at interview stage by short-listed firms*
- CMGC Management Price percentage (contractor's markup on construction costs)
- Preconstruction services fee
- Fee inclusions and exclusions

### 3.3 Price Proposal Content

**Envelope 2 shall contain:**
- Preconstruction services lump sum fee with task-category breakdown
- Key personnel hourly rates and estimated hours
- CM/GC management fee percentage with inclusions/exclusions clearly stated
- Assumptions regarding preconstruction duration and deliverables

## 4. Evaluation Criteria

### 4.1 Technical Evaluation (70% of total score)

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Key Personnel and Organization | 20% | Experience, qualifications, availability, team structure |
| Project Understanding | 10% | Depth of knowledge, risk awareness, stakeholder understanding |
| Project Approach | 20% | Feasibility, innovation, specificity, constructability input |
| Schedule | 10% | Realism, completeness, risk mitigation, milestone alignment |
| Risk Management | 5% | Comprehensiveness, specificity of mitigation, process |
| Safety | 5% | Record, project-specific plans, culture |
| Quality Management | 5% | Procedures, organization, documentation |
| Innovation | 5% | Value, feasibility, relevance to this project |

*Note: Percentages are of the total score (technical + price). Technical factors total 70%.*

### 4.2 Price Evaluation (20% of total score)

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Preconstruction Services Fee | 10% |
| CM/GC Management Fee Percentage | 10% |

Price Score = (Lowest Fee / Proposer's Fee) × Maximum Points

### 4.3 Interview (10% of total score)
- 45-minute presentation by key personnel
- 30-minute Q&A with Selection Panel
- Key personnel identified in the proposal must attend; substitutions will be penalized
- Evaluates team chemistry, communication, and depth of understanding

### 4.4 Total Score
```
Total Score = Technical Score (70%) + Price Score (20%) + Interview Score (10%)
```

### 4.5 Rating Scale
| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Exceptional (5) | Significantly exceeds requirements; innovative, very low risk |
| Good (4) | Exceeds requirements in meaningful ways; low risk |
| Acceptable (3) | Meets all requirements; manageable risk |
| Marginal (2) | Below requirements but correctable; notable gaps |
| Unacceptable (1) | Fails to meet requirements; disqualifying if not corrected |

## 5. Contract Terms

### 5.1 Preconstruction Contract
- Separate contract for CM preconstruction services
- Lump sum fee as proposed
- Duration: approximately 18 months or until CAP negotiation is complete
- NTP anticipated January 2022

### 5.2 Construction Contract (GC Phase)
- Executed only upon successful CAP negotiation
- GC CAP (Guaranteed Maximum Price) negotiated at approximately 90% design
- Open-book pricing required: CDOT has access to all cost data
- Self-performance minimum: 30% of construction value
- If CAP negotiation fails, CDOT reserves the right to competitively bid the construction work

### 5.3 Insurance and Bonding
- Performance and Payment Bonds: 100% of construction contract value
- Commercial General Liability: $2M per occurrence / $4M aggregate
- Automobile Liability: $2M combined single limit
- Workers' Compensation: Statutory limits
- Professional Liability: $2M per claim (for preconstruction design review services)

### 5.4 DBE Requirements
- Project DBE goal: 12.5% of construction contract value
- Good faith efforts documentation required
- DBE participation plan must be included in the proposal (Appendix D)
