## Safety Culture and Programs

### Leadership Commitment
Kraemer North America's safety program is managed by a dedicated corporate Safety Director who reports directly to the firm's President. Each project with a construction value exceeding $20M receives a full-time, on-site Safety Manager. This structure ensures safety decisions are not subordinated to production or schedule pressures.

### Training Requirements
- **Supervisory personnel:** OSHA 30-Hour Construction Safety required before assignment to any Kraemer project
- **All field workers:** OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety required; project-specific orientation completed before first day on site
- **Specialty certifications:** Blasting safety certification for all personnel involved in drill-and-blast operations; confined space entry certification for tunnel work; fall protection competent person certification for bridge crews

### Incident Prevention
- Daily pre-shift safety meetings (toolbox talks) tailored to the day's planned activities and specific hazards
- Weekly site safety inspections conducted by the project Safety Manager, with owner participation encouraged
- Near-miss reporting program with anonymous submission option and no-blame investigation
- Stop-work authority granted to every worker on site regardless of role or employer — any individual may halt operations for perceived unsafe conditions without fear of retaliation
- Monthly safety performance reports submitted to the project owner

### Emergency Response
- Site-specific emergency action plans developed for each project, reviewed quarterly
- Coordination with local emergency services (fire, EMS, law enforcement) before construction begins
- Emergency access routes maintained at all times during active construction, even during lane closures or traffic holds
- For mountain corridor projects: swift-water rescue equipment staged at creek access points; personnel trained in swift-water rescue techniques when working adjacent to waterways
