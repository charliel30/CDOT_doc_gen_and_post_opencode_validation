# I-70 Floyd Hill — Stakeholder Requirements and Concerns

*Backward-engineered from publicly available meeting notes, news coverage, community input, and agency correspondence. This document represents the stakeholder landscape a CM/GC proposer would need to understand.*

---

## CDOT (Project Owner)

### Primary Goals
- Add westbound Express Lane capacity to reduce recurring congestion
- Improve curve geometry from 45 mph to 55 mph design speed
- Complete westbound I-70 new alignment by end of 2027; full project by 2029
- Deliver within $905M adjusted budget
- Maintain I-70 mobility during construction (minimize delays and closures)
- Demonstrate successful CM/GC delivery to build confidence in alternative delivery methods

### Expectations of CM/GC Contractor
- Proactive constructability input that measurably improves the design
- Open-book transparency on all cost components
- Meet or beat schedule milestones
- Zero environmental compliance violations
- Strong safety record (zero lost-time incidents goal)
- Responsive communication and partnership, not adversarial relationship

---

## FHWA (Federal Highway Administration)

### Requirements
- NEPA compliance (FONSI conditions must be met throughout construction)
- Federal-aid compliance: DBE, Buy America, Davis-Bacon, Uniform Audit
- CM/GC procurement must follow 23 CFR Part 635 Subpart E
- Regular project oversight reviews
- Construction authorization requires successful CAP negotiation

### Concerns
- Ensuring the CM/GC process delivers value compared to traditional low-bid
- Cost growth beyond the original estimate (now $905M vs. $700M)
- Timely obligation of federal INFRA grant funds ($100M)

---

## Clear Creek County

### Authority
- 1041 permit authority over projects affecting areas of state interest
- Application submitted February 2025

### Requirements
- Detailed description of all mitigation measures
- Timeline for when mitigation will be implemented
- Explanation of how mitigation will be financed
- Ongoing compliance monitoring

### Concerns
- Traffic impacts on local roads (US 40, CR 65, US 6) during construction and detours
- Water quality in Clear Creek (the county's primary natural asset for tourism and recreation)
- Noise, dust, and vibration impacts on Floyd Hill and Idaho Springs residents
- Construction truck traffic on local roads
- Long-term maintenance of improvements

---

## City of Idaho Springs

### Concerns
- Economic impact on local businesses during multi-year construction
- Traffic congestion on local streets when I-70 is under construction
- Access to the city from I-70 during interchange reconstruction
- Noise and dust from nearby construction activities
- Desire for improved pedestrian/bicycle connectivity (Clear Creek Greenway Trail)

### Expectations
- Advance notice of closures and traffic pattern changes
- Shuttle service or alternative access during extended closures
- Construction scheduling that avoids peak tourism and event periods where possible
- Local hiring and business participation in construction

---

## Upper Clear Creek Watershed Association (UCCWA)

### Concerns
- Chloride levels in Clear Creek from highway deicing operations
- Sediment loading during construction earthwork
- Long-term stormwater runoff from expanded impervious surfaces
- Riparian habitat disturbance along Clear Creek corridor
- Adequacy of water quality monitoring program

### Requirements
- Continuous water quality monitoring upstream and downstream of construction
- Annual reporting of monitoring data
- Immediate notification of any discharge events
- Restoration of disturbed riparian areas to pre-construction or better condition

---

## SWEEP ITF (Sediment/Water/Erosion Protection Interagency Task Force)

### Role
- Interagency oversight of sediment, water quality, and erosion impacts

### Requirements
- Comprehensive erosion and sediment control plan
- Real-time turbidity monitoring during construction
- Compliance with all CDPS (Colorado Discharge Permit System) requirements
- Coordination of chloride management between construction operations and CDOT maintenance

---

## Colorado Parks and Wildlife

### Requirements
- Wildlife crossings at Genesee and Empire must be designed per CPW standards
- Deer fencing, escape ramps, and deer guards per specifications
- Monitoring of wildlife crossing effectiveness post-construction
- Seasonal construction restrictions near wildlife movement corridors during critical migration/breeding periods

### Expectations
- Coordination on wildlife crossing design during preconstruction
- Contractor environmental compliance manager with wildlife awareness
- Minimized lighting impacts near wildlife corridors during nightwork

---

## Colorado State Historic Preservation Office (SHPO)

### Requirements
- Section 106 compliance for any impacts to historic or archaeological resources
- Treatment plan for the identified archaeological site
- Memorandum of agreement (MOA) with SHPO before work proceeds in affected areas
- Monitoring during ground-disturbing activities in sensitive areas

---

## I-70 Coalition

### Role
- Advocacy and coordination organization for the I-70 Mountain Corridor

### Priorities
- Timely project delivery — Floyd Hill is a top coalition priority
- Multimodal improvements (transit, trail, broadband) integrated with highway construction
- Adequate funding to complete the project without scope reduction
- Public communication and transparency about construction impacts

---

## Traveling Public / Commuters

### Concerns
- Travel time reliability during construction
- Safety through work zones
- Adequate advance warning of closures, delays, and detour routes
- Weekend ski traffic not significantly impacted
- Summer recreation traffic accommodated

### What They Expect
- Real-time information (text alerts, message signs, travel time displays)
- Minimal full closures; nighttime-only when necessary
- Safe, well-signed work zones with clear lane markings
- Courtesy shuttle services during extended closures

---

## Local Residents (Floyd Hill, Idaho Springs)

### Concerns
- Construction noise (especially blasting) and vibration impacts on homes
- Dust from rock excavation and earthwork
- Property value impacts during multi-year construction
- Access disruptions to neighborhoods during interchange and ramp work
- Wildlife impacts (deer in residential areas displaced by fencing)

### Expectations
- Advance notification of blasting schedules
- Vibration monitoring near residences
- Dust suppression measures
- Construction hours limited to reasonable daytime periods (with exceptions for nightwork on I-70)
- A responsive point of contact for complaints
